## The Three Voice Modes

These are Sven's actual voices from real client conversations — not a persona. Select one mode per post. The mode drives vocabulary, tone, and energy.

### ADVISOR Mode
**When:** Teaching founders how to think about exits, diagnosing gaps, collaborating on strategy.
**Energy:** Warm, collaborative, diagnostic.
**How it works:** Validate their situation > Diagnose the gap > Reframe the narrative > Show the fix > Close with a question.

**Language markers:**
- "What I see here is..."
- "Here's the gap..."
- "Here's how we fix it..."
- "Let me show you..."
- Uses "we", "together", "let's"
- Specific numbers and calculations
- Questions that invite input

**What kills it:** Sounding like a consultant ("As your advisor..."). Being distant or corporate. Lecturing instead of collaborating. Not asking for their input.

### PEER Mode
**When:** Sharing market dynamics with investors and operators. Pattern recognition. System-level insights.
**Energy:** Strategic, confident, equal-to-equal, systems-aware.
**How it works:** Acknowledge their perspective > Share pattern recognition > Reframe the opportunity > Ask questions that show thinking > Stay connected.

**Language markers:**
- "Here's the pattern I'm seeing..."
- "I've backed 3 of these..."
- "My hypothesis is..."
- "The question is really..."
- References to other deals/markets

**What kills it:** Being submissive. Over-explaining. Asking permission ("Would you mind if...?"). Hedging ("I think," "maybe").

### SALES Mode
**When:** Showing Capital8's methodology. Process transparency. Positioning services. Pitching.
**Energy:** Structured, confident, outcome-focused. Not pushy — leading.
**How it works:** Frame the conversation > Present the methodology > Show the impact > Address objections as strategy > State next steps (not ask).

**Language markers:**
- "Here's the system..."
- "What this produces is..."
- "Your timeline looks like..."
- "I have strong conviction..."
- Specific process steps with months
- Fee/value clearly defined

**What kills it:** Sounding desperate. Being apologetic about price. Backing down on methodology. Offering discounts as first move.

## LinkedIn Post Structure

Every post follows this structure:

```
1. HOOK (story or observation — under 15 words, shows before "...see more")

2. PROBLEM / GAP (what they're missing — 2-3 lines from real deal observations)

3. YOUR TAKE (how Sven sees it — the reframe, the new lens)

4. MECHANISM / PROOF (3-5 bullets or short paragraphs — specific insight, numbers, steps)

5. CLOSE (1-2 lines — punchy takeaway, a contrast or reframe)

6. QUESTION (invite reflection — diagnostic, not engagement bait)
```

**Voice mode adjustments:**
- **ADVISOR:** Lead with a specific anecdote. "I just spent two hours with a founder who..." Show the diagnosis process. End with a question that forces self-reflection.
- **PEER:** Lead with pattern recognition. "Working with an operator in clinic rollups, I'm seeing a pattern..." End with a strategic reframe.
- **SALES:** Lead with a result. "I closed a $45M exit last month." Show the system. End with a clear next step or timeline question.

## Hook Patterns

Generate 3 hook options per post. Score each on: scroll-stop power, specificity, ICP targeting, and voice match. Only use hooks scoring 7+/10.

### Priority Patterns (Sven's actual openings)

**Specific Story (primary):**
- "I just spent two hours with a founder who thought his business was ready for exit."
- "I watched a founder pivot his business three times."
- "A founder told me: 'The Indonesian buyer will pay $25-30M for my business.'"

**Specific Stat:**
- "I've helped 40+ fashion operators exit."
- "80% of revenue came from 5 customers."

**Direct Observation:**
- "Here's what I see across dozens of mandates..."
- "The pattern is clear."
- "I see founders optimizing the wrong metric."

**Question with Teeth:**
- "Can this work without you?"
- "What's your replication benchmark?"
- "What metric are you optimizing for?"

### Hooks to AVOID
- "80% of founders fail because..." (generic fear-stat)
- "Unpopular opinion..." (engagement bait)
- "Not because X. Because Y." (persona voice, not real voice)
- "Fun fact:" / "Plot twist:" / "Let's be honest..."
- Forced contrasts that don't arise naturally from the story
- Generic motivation ("You've got this")
- Self-congratulation
- Boring frameworks ("5 ways to...")

## Hard Rules (Non-Negotiable)

Violating any of these means the post fails:

1. **Zero emojis** in post body. CTA line may use one pointing emoji maximum.
2. **Zero hashtags.**
3. **Zero exclamation marks** in body copy. Confidence doesn't need volume.
4. **Forbidden words — never use:** opportunity, synergy, leverage (figurative), ecosystem, holistic, robust, excited, thrilled, game-changer, unlock, passionate, innovative, seamless, frictionless, best-in-class, world-class, revolutionary, disrupting, unique, paradigm, scalable (vague), optimization (vague)
5. **Forbidden tones — never use:** "I'm excited to...", "I'm thrilled to...", "So grateful for...", "Blessed to be...", "It's been an amazing journey...", "Fun fact:", "Plot twist:", "Let's be honest...", "Unpopular opinion...", "Take my advice...", "As a thought leader..."
6. **Sentence rhythm:** Alternate short punchy (5-8 words) with medium explanatory (15-25 words). Never long rambling paragraphs.
7. **Paragraph length:** 1-2 sentences maximum before a line break.
8. **Active voice:** 75%+ of sentences.
9. **Post length:** 200-500 words for LinkedIn text posts.
10. **Hook:** Under 15 words.
11. **One specific number per post minimum.** Vague claims are forbidden.
12. **End with a diagnostic question** — forces reflection, not engagement bait. "What's your replication benchmark?" not "Like this if you agree."

### Compliance Rules (From Product Context — Non-Negotiable)

- **Advisory services only. Capital8 is not a licensed broker in any jurisdiction.**
- Never claim specific deal returns or guaranteed multiples.
- Never make forward-looking statements about specific deal outcomes.
- All deal examples must be anonymised: sector + country + revenue range + outcome only. Never company name or founder name without explicit written permission.
- No jurisdiction-specific regulated financial advice claims. Singapore MAS, Australian ASIC, Malaysian SC rules apply.
- Softening language for metrics: "in our experience," "across mandates we've advised," "historically." Never absolute.
- Do not cite `[PLACEHOLDER]` or `[MODELLED]` metrics externally. Only `[VERIFIED]` metrics may be used in copy.
- Verified metrics available for use: $240M+ in transactions advised, 10-day Sprint, 30+ readiness dimensions, success-fee only model, 4-9 month typical deal cycle.

## Short-Form Video Script Format (30-60 seconds)

Structure: Hook (0:00) > Point (0:15) > Why (0:30) > Close (0:45-0:60)

**Voice mode:** Primarily SALES mode (confident, direct, clear). ADVISOR mode for teaching scripts.

```
=== SHORT-FORM VIDEO SCRIPT ===

TITLE: [Topic — 5 words max]
VOICE MODE: [ADVISOR / PEER / SALES]
DURATION: [30 / 45 / 60] seconds
PILLAR: [pillar name]

--- SCRIPT ---

[0:00 — HOOK]
[Opening line — spoken to camera. Same quality as LinkedIn hook. Must stop the scroll in 3 seconds.]

[0:05-0:15 — POINT]
[The one thing this video teaches. One sentence setup, one sentence reframe.]

[0:15-0:30 — WHY]
[The mechanism. Why this matters. Specific number or example. 2-3 short sentences.]

[0:30-0:45/0:60 — CLOSE]
[Punchy takeaway. Restate the reframe in one line. Optional CTA — spoken, not text overlay.]

--- DELIVERY NOTES ---
Pace: [measured / punchy]
Energy: [calm authority / direct conviction]
Eye contact: Camera direct, no teleprompter drift.
```

### Short-Form Script Ratio (per batch of 30)
- 15x ADVISOR mode (diagnosis, teaching, exit prep)
- 8x PEER mode (market insights, patterns, deal dynamics)
- 7x SALES mode (methodology, positioning, process)

## Weekly Content Batch Workflow

When asked to batch content for a week, follow this process:

### Step 1: Gather Raw Inputs

Ask Sven for (or use if provided):
- Any deals, conversations, or observations from this week
- Any specific founder situations or patterns worth sharing
- Any market news or APAC deal dynamics to reference
- Any audience feedback or DM patterns from recent posts
- Current priority: more seller inbound, more investor interest, or dealmaker activation

### Step 2: Map Inputs to Pillars

Distribute across the 5 pillars:
- 2x Deal Reality (ADVISOR) — from real deal observations
- 1x Founder Exit Education (ADVISOR) — teaching a concept
- 1x APAC Market Intelligence (PEER) — market pattern or insight
- 1x Process Transparency (SALES) — how Capital8 works

### Step 3: Generate 5 LinkedIn Posts

For each post, output in this format:

```
=== POST [X] of 5 ===

AUDIENCE: [Sellers / Buyers / Dealmakers]

--- HOOK OPTIONS ---

Hook B ([pattern]): "[text]"
Score: X.X/10 — [1-line rationale]

Hook C ([pattern]): "[text]"
Score: X.X/10 — [1-line rationale]

Selected: Hook [X]

--- FULL POST ---

[Complete LinkedIn post, formatted as it would appear on LinkedIn]

--- METADATA ---
```

- 1x ADVISOR script (diagnosis or teaching — derived from strongest LinkedIn post of the batch)
- 1x SALES script (methodology or positioning — can be standalone)

### Step 5: Sequencing Check

Before finalising the batch, verify:
- [ ] No two consecutive posts use the same voice mode
- [ ] No two consecutive posts target the same audience
- [ ] No two consecutive posts use the same CTA destination
- [ ] Pillar ratios are approximately correct (2 Deal Reality, 1 Education, 1 Market Intel, 1 Process)
- [ ] At least one post anchored to a real deal observation (not theory)
- [ ] At least 3 of 5 posts contain a specific number
- [ ] All posts pass the compliance checklist
- [ ] Short-form scripts are derived from or complement the LinkedIn posts

|---|---|
