## Capital8 Seller-Facing Materials

### Objection Handling Framework

**Objection 1: "I already have a broker"**

Capital8 response: "That's common. What we're hearing from founders with brokers is one of two things: either they're in a traditional auction and wanted a second opinion on the process, or their broker is listing them broadly and they're concerned about confidentiality and market timing. Which of those sounds closer to your situation?"

Why this works: Acknowledges the broker without challenging the founder's judgment. Diagnoses whether the founder has real concerns (auction fatigue, market leak risk) or is just hedging. Opens a conversation about what's actually broken, not what's wrong with their current advisor.

---

**Objection 2: "I'm not ready yet"**

Capital8 response: "That's smart. Most founders aren't ready before they know what ready looks like. We built the ExitFit Diagnostic to answer that exact question — no obligation, takes 20 minutes. You'll get a score and see specifically which areas move the needle for your valuation and which don't. Then you know what to work on, and you know whether you're 6 months away or 24 months away. Does that help you plan?"

Why this works: Reframes "not ready" as lack of visibility, not lack of timing. Offers a diagnostic first, not a mandate pitch. Gives the founder control and clarity, which is what "not ready" founders actually want.

---

**Objection 3: "I'll wait for an offer to come in"**

Capital8 response: "I understand. Here's what we see happen: offers come in, but they're almost always inbound from buyers who already know the founder or the space. Those offers tend to be aggressive on price and come with a lot of structure (rollover, earnout, short collar). The founders who get the highest valuations and most control over terms are the ones who've been proactive — not because they needed to sell, but because they mapped out what their options actually were. An offer without options is usually a negotiation that's already decided."

Why this works: Doesn't push back on the founder's preference to wait. Instead, illustrates the real cost of waiting — you lose negotiating leverage. Uses pattern recognition (proof) rather than pressure. Founder recognizes the logic even if they still want to wait.

---

**Objection 4: "Your fee is too high"**

Capital8 response: "I get that. Let me tell you what we're different on: we don't charge retainer. You only pay us if we close a deal, and we only close deals where you're getting more value than if you'd done it yourself. That's the bet we make. If that math doesn't work for you — if you think you'd get the same outcome at a lower fee — then you might be right, and we wouldn't be a fit. But if you're curious whether we can move your valuation or help you structure a better deal than the offers you'd get, that's worth a conversation. What's the biggest concern — the dollar amount, or whether it's worth it?"

Why this works: Doesn't compete on price. Clarifies the model (success-based, not retainer). Turns the objection back to value, not cost. Lets the founder self-select out if they're shopping on price. Attracts founders who care about outcome.

---

**Objection 5: "I don't trust advisors"**

Capital8 response: "That's fair. Most of that comes from either a bad experience or watching someone else have one. What would it take for you to trust that someone in this space actually has your interests ahead of their commission? Is it track record, is it seeing how they approach the first conversation, or is it something else?"

Why this works: Validates the objection without defending the industry. Asks an honest diagnostic question. Moves toward evidence (track record, first conversation proof) rather than promises. Keeps the founder in control of the decision.

### Pitch Structure

The Capital8 sales conversation follows three moves:

1. **Diagnosis (ADVISOR voice)**: Ask about the founder's current situation, timeline, who they've talked to, what they know about their valuation. Don't pitch. Listen for hesitation, uncertainty, fear of giving it away, or broker dissatisfaction. Take notes.

2. **Off-Market Positioning**: "Most founders are thinking about a traditional sale — you put the company on the market, get offers, pick the best one. We think differently. We start by finding the right buyer for your specific situation, not all buyers. That usually means off-market conversations first, which means better confidentiality, better terms, and you're not racing against other bids. But it also means we need to know exactly what you're optimizing for — is it price, is it liquidity, is it staying involved post-close?"

3. **Creative Deal Structures**: "And because we work with a network of financial buyers and strategic partners who think differently, we can often structure a deal in a way a traditional broker can't. Earnout, seller financing, rollover equity, hybrid approaches. The right structure can add 10-20% to your take-home if it's designed for you, not against you."

End with proof: "We've closed over $240M in exits. The average founder we work with doesn't do a traditional IPO or full sale. They do something in between — off-market, structured for their specific goals. Let's figure out what makes sense for you."

### Follow-Up Sequence Triggers

Send what, when, and to whom:

- **Day 1 (immediately after conversation)**: Email confirming what you heard + "Here's what I'd recommend we explore next" + link to ExitFit Score. No hard CTA. Tone: ADVISOR. Subject: "Next steps — [Founder Name]"

- **Day 3 (if no response)**: One email with a specific question about their business (revenue, EBITDA range, owner distribution) framed as "to give you the right guidance." No sales pitch. Tone: PEER. Subject: "One more question re: your exit planning"

- **Day 10 (if still no response)**: A closed deal story (anonymised) that mirrors their profile — same revenue range, same industry challenge, same initial hesitation, how it resolved. Tone: PEER. Subject: "Ran into a pattern you might recognize"

- **Day 30 (if no engagement)**: Check-in. "We've sent a few things your way. Where you at with this? No pressure — just want to know if this is still on the radar or if something changed." Short. Tone: PEER.

If the founder schedules an ExitFit or Sprint after any of these emails, the sequence stops and transitions to the post-Sprint nurture sequence (see email-sequence-capital8 skill).

## SevenX Capital Investor-Facing Materials

### Deal Summary Template Structure

Investor materials follow this structure. Never deviate:

**Header**: Deal name (anonymised), sector, geography, seller/buyer profile (one line each)

**Investment Thesis** (4-5 sentences): Why this deal, why now, what makes it fundable, what returns are possible. PEER voice. No hype.

**Deal Metrics**:
- Revenue (TTM)
- EBITDA (TTM) and margin
- Entry valuation / Revenue multiple
- Projected exit multiple (range)
- Projected holding period
- Projected IRR (range)

**Structure**: What is SevenX actually investing in? (Equity, preferred, debt, hybrid?) What are the terms? (Liquidation preference, board seat, protective provisions?)

**Use of Capital**: What is the money for? (Growth capex, working capital, acquisition, dividend to seller?)

**Key Risks**: 3-4 material risks, not downside scenarios. Tone: honest, not alarming.

**Operator / Management**: One paragraph. Track record, relevant experience, alignment with SevenX thesis.

**Next Steps**: What's the timeline? When do you need commitments? How will you allocate capital?

**Appendix**: Cap table, financial statements (2-3 years), key customer/revenue concentration, customer contracts, management resumes.

Rule: Never include founder personal information, owner confidentiality, or unaudited financial detail in the deck header. Mark as "Confidential — SevenX Capital Partners" and route via separate secure channel.

### Investor Objection Responses

**Objection 1: "Show me the track record"**

SevenX response: "We track returns by vintage year and strategy. We can share the summary — [produce Capital8 returns by year, what percentage of deals hit 3x+, what percentage failed, average hold period]. What we don't do is cherry-pick winners. The track record includes everything we've funded. We're happy to walk through specific deals that are relevant to your investment thesis if that helps."

Why this works: Leads with data, not stories. Acknowledges the request honestly. Offers specificity (deal review) for investors who want deeper digging.

---

**Objection 2: "What's the minimum commitment?"**

SevenX response: "Depends on the deal. Some opportunities are smaller ($500K-$2M check sizes) and others are larger. We typically manage capital as a syndicate, so you can size to what makes sense for you. There's no house minimum. What size range are you thinking, and what kind of deal profile interests you most? That tells us what opportunities to bring to you."

Why this works: Makes it clear this isn't a closed fund. Frames sizing flexibility. Asks a qualifying question to understand investor motivation.

---

**Objection 3: "How is this different from buying a company myself?"**

SevenX response: "Three things. First, you don't have to find the deal — that's our job, and we have access to off-market flow that doesn't make it to traditional brokers. Second, you're not alone on operations — there's a management team and Capital8 advising the growth plan, which reduces your personal workload. Third, you're de-risked by having multiple investors; if something goes wrong, you're not the only person holding the bag. If you want to do it solo and have the bandwidth to find a good target, you probably should. If you want off-market access without taking on operational risk, this is different."

Why this works: Doesn't oversell. Clearly distinguishes SevenX syndication from traditional SMB acquisition. Lets investor self-select based on preference and resources.

## Confidentiality Framework

Capital8 operates on a multi-stage information release model. The principle: what gets shared depends on the stage of the relationship and the legitimacy of the prospect.

**Stage 0 — First Contact**: Public-facing information only. ExitFit Score materials, website copy, general Capital8 process. No deal information. No founder names. No financial detail beyond methodology.

**Stage 1 — Qualified Interest (Post-ExitFit, Pre-Sprint)**: Anonymised market patterns. "We're seeing multiples in SaaS ranging from 6-8x, down from 8-10x 18 months ago." No specific deal information. Founder can share their company name and rough financials; Capital8 keeps this confidential and doesn't circulate without permission.

**Stage 2 — Sprint / Engagement Period**: Specific deal context (sector trends, comparable transactions, buyer behaviour in the founder's space). Still no other founders' names or financials. Founder information is shared only with Capital8 team and pre-vetted financial partners under NDA.

**Stage 3 — LOI / Formal Process**: Full deal summary, investor introductions, buyer short list. All parties under written NDAs. Deal room is created with role-based access controls.

**Stage 4 — Post-Close**: Case study rights negotiated on deal-by-deal basis. Default is confidential unless founder explicitly opts into being named as a reference.

## Voice Rules for Sales Materials

**For seller-facing content (ADVISOR mode)**:
- Opener: diagnosis, not pitch. "Tell me about your situation."
- No urgency framing. Never "You should sell now" or "Multiples are declining." Facts are okay; pressure is not.
- Specificity over enthusiasm. Reference actual data, actual deals (anonymised), actual buyer behaviour.
- No superlatives. Never "best," "top," "leading," "only," "revolutionary."
- Active voice. 75% minimum. "We help founders structure deals" not "Founders are helped to structure deals."
- No forbidden vocabulary: opportunity, synergy, leverage, ecosystem, holistic, robust, excited, thrilled, game-changer, unlock, unique, revolutionary, innovative, seamless, frictionless.

**For investor-facing content (PEER mode)**:
- Opening: context, not credential. "Here's what we're seeing in market."
- Confidence without arrogance. Present facts and reasoning; let investor draw conclusions.
- Specificity required. Vague deal summaries lose credibility instantly.
- Active voice. 75%+ minimum.
- No forbidden vocabulary: same list as above, plus "opportunity set," "attractive risk-return," "uncorrelated," "alpha generation."
- Acknowledge risks honestly. Omitting risks signals incompetence or hidden deals.
- No exclamation marks. Period. Ever.

This skill produces three main outputs:

2. **Follow-Up Email** (100-200 words): One email from the sequence, with subject line, body, and CTA. Ready to send or adapt. No personalization tokens — those are filled in by the sender.
