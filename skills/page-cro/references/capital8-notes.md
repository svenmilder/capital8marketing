# Capital8 CRO Reference Notes

These notes extend the page-cro skill with Capital8-specific conversion principles. Load `.agents/product-marketing-context.md` before applying any of these.

---

## The High-Trust, Long-Cycle Conversion Model

Capital8 does not convert like SaaS. There is no free trial, no instant signup, no "start using it in 5 minutes." The conversion cycle is 6-36 months. The founder is deciding whether to sell the business they built over 5-20 years. Every page must account for this.

### What this means for CRO:

**Conversion goal is micro, not macro.** The macro conversion (signed mandate) takes months. The micro conversion on any page is one of:
- Complete the ExitFit Diagnostic (sellers) — 12 minutes
- Complete AcquireFit (buyers) — 10 minutes
- Apply to the Dealmaker Network (dealmakers)

**Friction reduction has limits.** Removing all friction (no forms, no questions, no qualification) would attract unqualified traffic. Capital8 intentionally qualifies through the diagnostic. The diagnostic IS the funnel — it self-selects serious founders and disqualifies tyre-kickers. CRO should optimise for diagnostic completion rate, not for raw signups.

**Trust signals matter more than urgency.** Founders in the consideration window respond to:
- Proof of closed deals (anonymised: sector + country + revenue + outcome)
- Process transparency (Room 0-4, Sprint timeline, fee structure)
- Disqualification (showing who Capital8 does NOT serve builds more trust than showing who it does)
- Peer credibility (Goldman-grade presentation, not startup energy)

**Urgency language is forbidden.** No "limited time," "act now," "spots filling up." The only legitimate urgency is market-driven: "macro conditions shift," "multiples compress." Use data, not pressure.

## The Three-Audience Navigation Challenge

Capital8 serves three audiences with fundamentally different needs, different entry points, and different conversion goals. All three share one domain and one navigation.

### Routing Rules for CRO:

| Audience | Entry CTA | Primary Page | What They Need First |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sellers | ExitFit Diagnostic | /seller | "Am I ready to sell?" — answered in 12 min |
| Buyers | AcquireFit | /buyers | "What deals are available?" — answered after AcquireFit |
| Dealmakers | Network Application | /dealmakers | "How do I earn from my relationships?" — answered on page |

**The homepage must route, not convert.** A cold visitor landing on the homepage doesn't know if they're a seller, buyer, or dealmaker. The homepage's job is to identify their intent and route them to the right page — not to convert them directly. The /dealready three-way chooser is the architectural solution to this.

**Every page should serve exactly one audience.** Cross-audience pages dilute conversion. The homepage is the only page where multi-audience content is acceptable, and even there, it should be a routing mechanism, not a pitch.

**Nav must not assume audience.** The global "Get Started" button should route to /dealready (the audience router), not to any single audience flow. "Login" is audience-neutral and correct.

**Footer is the safety net.** All three audiences should find their entry point in the footer, labelled clearly: "For Sellers," "For Buyers," "Dealmakers."

## The Diagnostic as Primary CTA

The ExitFit Diagnostic (sellers) and AcquireFit (buyers) are the primary conversion mechanisms for Capital8. They are not lead magnets — they are qualification instruments that produce a scored output.

### CRO implications:

**Every seller-facing page should CTA to /dealready.**
- Not to "Contact us"
- Not to "Book a call"
- Not to "Learn more"
- The diagnostic IS the first step. Everything else follows from the score.

**CTA copy should communicate the value of the diagnostic, not the action of taking it.**
- Weak: "Take the Diagnostic" / "Sign Up" / "Get Started"
- Strong: "Check Your ExitFit" / "See Where You Stand" / "Discover Your Exit Readiness"
- The output (score, readiness assessment, gap identification) is the value — not the input (filling out a form).

**Time and commitment framing matters.**
- Always include: "12 minutes" (seller) or "10 minutes" (buyer)
- Always include: "Free" or "No commitment"
- Consider adding: "No advisor call required" — reduces friction for founders who aren't ready to talk

**One CTA copy per page.** When the same page has 5 different button labels all going to /dealready, it creates cognitive load. Standardise to one primary CTA copy per page. Repeat it at the hero, mid-page, and closing — same words each time.

**Post-diagnostic is where the real conversion happens.** The diagnostic produces a score. The score creates a natural next step (Track A: Sprint, or Track B: Readiness). CRO after the diagnostic is about converting scores into booked calls. This is seller-nurture-email territory, not page-cro.

## Compliance Constraints on Page Claims

Capital8 is an advisory firm, not a licensed broker. All page copy must observe these constraints. These are not style preferences — they are regulatory requirements.

### Hard Rules:

1. **Never guarantee returns, multiples, or deal outcomes.** No "sell for 6x EBITDA." No "guaranteed premium." No specific return projections.

2. **Never make forward-looking statements about specific deals.** "This mandate will close at $X" is never acceptable on any page.

3. **All deal examples must be anonymised.** Format: sector + country + revenue range + outcome. Never company name, founder name, or buyer name without explicit written permission.

4. **Only [VERIFIED] metrics may appear on external pages.** If a number isn't verified in the product context, it cannot appear on the website. Phrases like "in our experience" and "across mandates we've advised" provide appropriate softening.

5. **"Advisory services only. Not a licensed broker."** This footer line must appear on every page. It is not optional.

6. **Room 0-4 confidentiality must be enforced on the site itself.** Deal cards visible to unauthenticated visitors must show Room 0 information only: sector, geography, revenue range. No company names, no specific financials, no founder details.

### Forbidden Vocabulary on Pages:
opportunity, synergy, leverage (figurative), ecosystem, holistic, robust, excited, thrilled, game-changer, unlock, passionate, innovative, seamless, frictionless, best-in-class, world-class, revolutionary, disrupting

### Brand Terms (exact capitalisation required):
- ExitFit Diagnostic (never "quiz" or "survey")
- ExitFit Score (never "result" or "grade")
- ExitFit Sprint (capital S)
- Mandate (never "listing" or "deal" in seller-facing copy)
- Mandate Desk (capital M, capital D)
- Room 0-4 (not "data room stages")
- Capital8 (no space, never "Capital 8")
- Success fee (never "commission")

### Regulatory Context:
Singapore MAS, Australian ASIC, and Malaysian SC rules apply to all external materials. When in doubt, soften. "In our experience across mandates" is always safer than "Capital8 delivers X."

## Page-Specific CRO Notes

### Homepage
- Job: Route cold visitors to the right audience page
- Primary test: Does an audience router above the fold improve downstream diagnostic completions?
- Key metric: Click-through to /seller, /buyers, or /dealmakers (not bounce rate)

### Seller Page (/seller)
- Job: Convince a founder to spend 12 minutes on the ExitFit Diagnostic
- Primary test: Shorter page (hero + why exits fail + CTA + proof) vs current long page
- Key metric: ExitFit Diagnostic starts from this page
- Note: The "Why Exits Fail" section is the highest-value content — it should never be removed or deprioritised

### Buyers Page (/buyers)
- Job: Convince a buyer to complete AcquireFit
- Primary test: Deal cards visible to all vs gated behind AcquireFit
- Key metric: AcquireFit completions from this page
- Note: Deal card quality is critical — empty or placeholder cards destroy credibility

### Dealmakers Page (/dealmakers)
- Job: Get a professional to apply to the network
- Primary test: 3-track structure is working — test track-specific landing pages
- Key metric: Dealmaker applications (referral, co-advisory, territory)
- Note: This is the best-structured page on the site — optimise, don't redesign

### Pricing Pages
- Job: Remove price as an objection, move to signup
- Primary test: Recommended plan badge on Mandate Desk Starter
- Key metric: Clicks from pricing to application/signup flow
- Note: Fee transparency builds trust — never obscure pricing in this business

### /dealready (Diagnostic Entry)
- Job: Get the visitor into the right diagnostic flow
- Primary test: Add trust signals vs current clean design
- Key metric: Diagnostic completion rate (not just starts)
- Note: This page should be the destination of "Get Started" in the nav
