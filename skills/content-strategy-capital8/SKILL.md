## The Three-Audience Model

Capital8 content strategy segments into three distinct audiences. Rule: never conflate. Each piece of content has one audience only.

### Audience 1: Capital8 Sellers

**Profile**: Founders and business owners, age 35-65, owner-operated businesses, first-time or early-exit experience, revenue $2M-$25M, real EBITDA. Geographic distribution: anywhere Capital8 operates.

**Goal**: ExitFit Score completion → Sprint booking → Mandate signature → Close

**Mindset**: "I don't want to give it away." "I've had offers but don't know if they're real." "I've never done this before." "My broker just wanted to list me." Skeptical of advisors, protective of business, thinking long-term, not rushing.

**Content needs**: Education on what buyers look for, diagnosis of their specific business position, pattern recognition (other deals that look like theirs), framework for thinking through timing and structure. No urgency, high specificity.

**Volume**: 70% of all content month-over-month.

---

### Audience 2: SevenX Capital Investors

**Profile**: HNW individuals, family offices, search operators, principal-led acquisition funds. Accredited, investment-experienced, looking for off-market deal flow with Capital8 curated due diligence.

**Goal**: deals@sevenx.capital inbound → investor qualification → deal summaries shared → fund commits → deal closes

**Mindset**: "Show me the deal." "What's my role post-acquisition?" "How is this different from doing a search myself?" "What's the capital efficiency?" Analytical, professional, high-volume deal exposure, looking for off-market advantage.

**Content needs**: Deal flow updates, track record proof, investor thesis clarity, market patterns relevant to their thesis, case examples of exits that returned 3x+. No marketing speak, all evidence-based.

**Volume**: 20% of all content month-over-month.

---

### Audience 3: Dealmaker Network

**Profile**: Accountants, tax attorneys, CFAs, wealth managers, search operators, past clients. Professional advisors or deal sourcing professionals with deal flow access and network.

**Goal**: Referral activation → qualified submissions → closed deal commissions

**Mindset**: "Send me the right deal, not all deals." "Respect my time." "Show me patterns so I can spot them in my network." Professional, busy, selective, looking for easy-to-execute referral process.

**Content needs**: Spotting signals (how to recognize exit-ready founders), market patterns, case studies (anonymised outcomes), economics and process clarity. No hype, all actionable.

**Volume**: 10% of all content month-over-month.

## Voice Allocation Framework

Monthly content mix follows this structure:

| Voice Mode | % of Monthly Content | Purpose | Audience Mix |
|---|---|---|---|
| ADVISOR (teaching, diagnosis) | 40% | Build authority, teach founder thinking | 70% seller, 20% investor, 10% network |
| PEER (patterns, market insight) | 30% | Establish credibility through observation | 60% seller, 30% investor, 10% network |
| SALES (methodology, process) | 20% | Convert engaged prospects to action | 80% seller, 15% investor, 5% network |
| BRAND (proof, credentials) | 10% | Authority, track record, no CTA | 50% seller, 40% investor, 10% network |

**Audience allocation across a month**: 70% seller-facing, 20% investor-facing, 10% network-facing in total volume.

**Why this ratio**: Sellers have longer consideration cycles (6-36 months). They need repeated teaching to build confidence and trust before they're ready to engage. Investors have higher transaction velocity; less content needed to move them. Network members are passive until they see a deal; lower volume.

## The No-Urgency Constraint

Capital8's ICP makes major financial decisions over 12-36 month horizons. Founders are typically non-linear in their engagement and often don't act on time horizons that pressure tactics assume.

### What Doesn't Work for Capital8

- Limited-time offers: "Only 3 slots available this quarter"
- FOMO-based framing: "Multiples are dropping, act now"
- Scarcity messaging: "Few off-market deals at this valuation"
- Urgency language: "Don't miss this window"
- Countdown timers or deadline-based CTA

All of these are brand-damaging for Capital8 because:

1. **Ineffective**: The ICP doesn't respond to urgency for long-cycle decisions
2. **Credibility loss**: Urgent messaging signals commodity business (deal volume) not advisory business (quality thinking)
3. **Attracts wrong founder**: Pressure-responsive founders make rushed decisions and become difficult clients later

### What Actually Works for Capital8

- **Specificity**: "Working capital efficiency moves valuation 0.8x in your space" (specific, factual, founder-actionable)
- **Pattern recognition**: "We're seeing buyer consolidation in [sector], which changes who has buy-in power" (founder learns)
- **Cost of a bad decision**: "Most seller regret comes from not understanding what creative structure could have added to their take-home" (consequence, not pressure)
- **Market observation**: "Multiples in manufacturing have compressed 0.8x in 18 months" (statement of fact, founder draws own timing conclusion)
- **Advisory framing**: "Here's how to think about the decision" (methodology, not manipulation)

**Rule**: No post should create artificial urgency. Content is allowed to reference market timing as a factual observation, not as a pressure tactic. Founder reads it, thinks "This is useful context," and moves at their own pace.

## Content Pillar Framework

Capital8 content organizes around five pillars, each serving a specific audience need.

### Pillar 1: Exit Education (30% of monthly content)

**Purpose**: Teach founders how to think about exit planning, what buyers look for, what structures are available

**Audience**: 90% seller-facing

**Voice mode**: ADVISOR (teaching)

**Example topics**:
- "What buyers actually look for in your revenue range"
- "How to think about earnout versus cash at close"
- "Working capital: why it matters, how it affects your valuation"
- "Why management depth above the founder adds 1-2x to your multiple"
- "The real cost of customer concentration"

**Output**: LinkedIn post + email nurture sequence + one-pager reference guide

---

### Pillar 2: Mistake Diagnosis (25% of monthly content)

**Purpose**: Help founders recognize common errors before they make them, build trust through pattern recognition

**Audience**: 85% seller-facing, 10% network-facing

**Voice mode**: PEER (pattern recognition)

**Example topics**:
- "The broker trap: why traditional brokers leave money on the table"
- "Valuation inflation: why founders overestimate multiples"
- "The timing mistake: waiting for an offer instead of mapping options"
- "Management dependency: why buyers discount heavily when founder is single point of failure"
- "Customer concentration: how losing one customer can tank your deal"

**Output**: LinkedIn case study + email + podcast/interview

---

### Pillar 3: Market Patterns (20% of monthly content)

**Purpose**: Establish credibility through direct market observation, give sellers and investors useful context

**Audience**: 60% seller-facing, 30% investor-facing, 10% network-facing

**Voice mode**: PEER (market insight)

**Example topics**:
- "Q1 2026 M&A activity: consolidation vs. private equity interest by sector"
- "Multiple compression in SaaS: what's driving buyer caution?"
- "Buyer behavior trends: strategic buyers vs. financial buyers in [space]"
- "Financing environment: how debt availability is changing deal structure options"

**Output**: LinkedIn post + market report email + investor briefing

---

### Pillar 4: Proof and Methodology (15% of monthly content)

**Purpose**: Demonstrate Capital8 expertise through specific examples, explain process credibly

**Audience**: 70% seller-facing, 20% investor-facing, 10% network-facing

**Voice mode**: SALES (methodology) + BRAND (credentials)

**Example topics**:
- "Why off-market first: the hidden leverage in confidential process"
- "Creative structures: how earnout + seller finance can add $X to your take-home"
- "The Capital8 process: ExitFit → Sprint → Mandate → Close (and why each step matters)"
- "Track record: $240M+ closed, here's what the outcomes actually look like"

**Output**: LinkedIn post + one-pager + case study (anonymised)

---

### Pillar 5: Investor Access (10% of monthly content)

**Purpose**: Promote SevenX Capital deal flow model to investors, build investor thesis clarity

**Audience**: 70% investor-facing, 20% seller-facing (education on why off-market access exists), 10% network-facing

**Voice mode**: PEER + SALES

**Example topics**:
- "Off-market deal flow: why 60% of good deals never hit the market"
- "SevenX model: how syndication reduces acquisition risk"
- "The operator thesis: why founder alignment creates better returns"
- "Investor outcomes: 3x+ returns, average hold 4-6 years, sector diversification"

**Output**: LinkedIn post + investor briefing + SevenX website content

## Channel-to-Audience Mapping

### LinkedIn (Primary Channel)

**Usage**: All three audiences, differentiated post types and messaging

- **Sellers**: Educational posts (Pillar 1), mistake diagnostics (Pillar 2), founder-centric CTA (ExitFit Score link)
- **Investors**: Market patterns (Pillar 3), proof posts (Pillar 4), investor-centric CTA (deals@sevenx.capital)
- **Network**: Pattern spotting (Pillar 2), methodologies (Pillar 4), network-centric CTA (referral link or calendar)

**Posting frequency**: 4 posts per week, rotating audience and voice mode

**Format**: Long-form posts (350-600 words), no images (text-only for engagement and archive SEO), one CTA per post

---

### Email (Segmented by List)

**List 1 — Seller nurture** (post-ExitFit non-converts)
- Frequency: 6 emails over 90 days (sequence per email-sequence-capital8 skill)
- Content: Educational (Pillar 1) + patterns (Pillar 2 + 3) + direct ask
- CTA: Sprint booking, calendar link

**List 2 — Post-Sprint nurture** (mandate non-converts)
- Frequency: 4 emails over 60 days
- Content: Educational + specific action items + market updates + soft check-in
- CTA: Soft ask or "reply to update me"

**List 3 — Investor updates**
- Frequency: 2x per month
- Content: Deal flow updates, market patterns (Pillar 3), investor outcome proof (Pillar 4)
- CTA: deals@sevenx.capital, calendar link

**List 4 — Network member engagement**
- Frequency: 1x per month
- Content: Spotting signals, market patterns, referral encouragement
- CTA: Referral form or reply-to-submit

---

### SEO / Programmatic (Seller-Facing Only)

**Target audience**: Founder actively searching for M&A advisor information

**Keywords**: "How to sell my business," "Business exit planning," "What is my business worth," "M&A advisor [city]," "Business valuation calculator"

**Content type**: Long-form educational posts (1,500-2,500 words), optimized for search intent

**CTA**: ExitFit Score calculator link, "Get your free exit readiness assessment"

**Frequency**: 2-4 posts per month, depending on keyword research

---

### Paid Advertising (Seller-Facing, High-Intent Keywords Only)

**Platform**: LinkedIn Ads only. Not brand awareness. Only high-intent keywords.

**Target audience**: Founders in target industries, revenue band, who have engaged with exit-planning content in last 30 days

**Keywords**: Exact match only. "Exit advisor [city]," "Business M&A [state]," "How to structure a business sale"

**Creative**: Ad copy = first 3 lines of educational post + CTA to ExitFit

**Budget allocation**: Allocate based on conversion rates. Pause underperforming ad sets within 7 days.

---

### Excluded Channels

**Instagram, TikTok, Twitter**: Wrong ICP. Capital8 founders and investors are not on these platforms seeking this information. Brand awareness there is wasted spend.

**Podcast (Capital8 as guest)**: Valuable only if podcast audience is target founders or investors. Selective participation if opportunity aligns.

**Video content (YouTube, Loom)**: Minimal. Founders prefer written content for exit planning (async, searchable, reference-able). Only produce video if interviewee or case study is high-profile.

## Content Calendar Principles

### Weekly Structure

**Monday**: PEER post (market pattern or case study) — targets investor or network audience slightly

**Wednesday**: ADVISOR post (teaching or diagnosis) — targets seller audience

**Friday**: SALES post (methodology, process, CTA-focused) — targets seller or investor conversion

**One more post per week** (timing varies): BRAND post (proof, credentials) or rotation post if previous week was light

Total: 4 posts per week, rotating voice modes, never two consecutive posts to same audience.

---

### Monthly Rhythm

- **Week 1**: Focus on market intelligence (Pillar 3). Give investors and network members fresh context.
- **Week 2**: Focus on education (Pillar 1). Teach sellers.
- **Week 3**: Focus on mistakes/patterns (Pillar 2). Build credibility through recognition.
- **Week 4**: Focus on proof and methodology (Pillar 4 + 5). Drive conversion from warm leads.

---

### Real Deal Data Integration

Once per week, anchor one post in real deal data from Capital8 database:

- "Just closed a deal in [sector] where [outcome]"
- "We're seeing [pattern] in active deals this quarter"
- "Buyer behavior in [space]: [observation from recent conversations]"

This keeps content specific, credible, and grounded. No made-up examples. No generic advice.

**Process**: Query Capital8 database for anonymised deal snapshot each Monday. Identify 1-2 insights. Write post from there.

---

### CTA Rotation

Never two consecutive posts with same CTA:

- Post 1: "Complete your ExitFit Score" (link)
- Post 2: No CTA, just teaching (or "Reply with questions")
- Post 3: "Schedule a Sprint" (calendar link)
- Post 4: "Get investor access to our deal flow" (SevenX info link)

This prevents CTA fatigue and tests which messages drive actual engagement.

---

### Monthly Performance Review

**Metrics to track** (via LinkedIn analytics + email + UTM tracking):

- **Engagement**: Likes, comments, shares per post type and pillar
- **Reach**: Impressions by audience (use LinkedIn targeting data to infer)
- **Conversion**: Clicks to ExitFit, Sprint bookings, investor inbound (via UTM or email tracking)
- **Pipeline**: Which content types show up most in closed deals? (via Salesforce/CRM cross-reference)

**Monthly action**:

1. Pull Query 10 (LinkedIn attribution) and identify top 3 performing content types by conversion rate
2. Increase that content type next month (e.g., if PEER pattern posts drive 20% Sprint bookings vs. 10% for ADVISOR posts, shift mix slightly toward Pillar 2 and 3)
3. Pause or retool content types that don't move conversion (e.g., if Pillar 5 investor posts get zero investor inbound, kill it for a month and test a different approach)

## What the Skill Produces

### 1. Monthly Content Calendar

A 4-week calendar with:
- **Date**: Publish date (day of week)
- **Topic**: Specific post title or theme
- **Pillar**: Which of the five pillars
- **Voice mode**: ADVISOR, PEER, SALES, or BRAND
- **Audience**: Primary audience (seller, investor, or network)
- **CTA**: Link destination or type of call-to-action
- **Type**: LinkedIn post, email, SEO piece, etc.

Example row: "Monday, Week 1 | 'Q1 2026 M&A Activity: Who's Buying Now' | Pillar 3 | PEER | 60% seller / 30% investor | deals@sevenx.capital | LinkedIn post"

Output: Spreadsheet or document, 30-40 rows, ready for team execution

---

### 2. Content Brief (Individual Piece)

Template for any single piece of content:

- **Topic**: Working title
- **Pillar & Voice**: Which pillar, what voice mode
- **Audience**: Who is this for (seller, investor, or network)
- **Objective**: What do we want the reader to think, feel, or do?
- **Key points**: 3-4 specific insights or arguments to hit
- **CTA**: What's the ask? (Sprint booking, ExitFit Score, deals@sevenx.capital, reply, etc.)
- **Tone notes**: Specific voice rules for this piece
- **Length**: Target word count
- **Real deal data**: Any anonymised deal or market data to ground this in truth

Output: 1-page brief, ready for writer or internal creator

---

### 3. Channel Strategy for Campaign or Launch

For any specific push (e.g., "Launch SevenX investor program" or "Reactivate dormant Sprint leads"):

- **Campaign name**: What are we launching?
- **Audience**: Who's the primary target?
- **Duration**: 30, 60, or 90 days?
- **Content pillars**: Which 2-3 pillars will dominate?
- **Channel sequencing**: What order? (e.g., LinkedIn warm-up → email → paid ad → direct outreach)
- **Success metrics**: How do we measure this campaign?
- **Budget/resource**: Headcount, design, paid spend, timeline

Output: 1-2 page plan, executable by team
