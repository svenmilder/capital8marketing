## The Three-Track Structure

Capital8 manages referrals through three distinct tracks, each tailored to the referrer's role, involvement level, and economics.

### Track 1: Dealmaker

**Who**: Active deal sourcing professionals, search operators, principal-led buyers, past Capital8 clients. People with direct market access and deal flow.

**Involvement**: High. Dealmakers are expected to source 3-6 qualified referrals per year. They work closely with Capital8 on deal structure, buyer profiles, and valuation strategy.

**What a qualified referral looks like**: Founder with $5M-$25M EBITDA, owner-operated, real profitability, genuine 12-36 month exit window, no broker engagement yet. Dealmakers are bringing deals with real momentum, not just leads.

**What the referrer does**: Source founder, brief Capital8 on founder's situation, make warm introduction, facilitate initial Sprint. Dealmaker becomes embedded in deal process and provides ongoing context to Capital8 and founder.

**What Capital8 does**: Everything else. ExitFit, Sprint, process management, buyer sourcing, transaction advisory, deal structuring, legal coordination.

### Track 2: Connector

**Who**: Sales people, consultants, angel investors, professionals with broad networks but not direct deal sourcing focus. People who naturally meet founders and can recognize signals.

**Involvement**: Low. Connectors are expected to submit 1-2 warm introductions per quarter based on casual network activity. No ongoing deal involvement required.

**What a qualified referral looks like**: Founder with $2M-$25M revenue, real EBITDA, ownership to sell, any timeline within 36 months. Connectors are introducing people they naturally know who fit the profile — they're not hunting.

**What the referrer does**: Identify founder, make warm introduction via email (CCing Capital8 or requesting intro permission), and done. No ongoing involvement necessary.

**What Capital8 does**: Everything. ExitFit, Sprint, process, due diligence, buyer sourcing, deal structuring.

Example: Consultant knows founder through existing client network, thinks they might be exit-ready, sends warm email introducing them to Capital8. If deal closes, Connector receives commission, which is lower than Dealmaker track but requires zero ongoing work.

---

### Track 3: Advisor

**Who**: Accountants, tax attorneys, CFAs, wealth managers, business coaches. Professional advisors in trusted relationship with business owners.

**Involvement**: Very low. Advisors reference-check referrals, not source them. They encounter founders in their regular advisory work, recognize readiness signals, and make introductions.

**What a qualified referral looks like**: Founder with $2M-$25M revenue, real EBITDA, genuine exit interest, often pre-realization that they should talk to an M&A advisor (your job is recognition, not recruitment). Compliance-sensitive — these are existing client relationships.

**What the referrer does**: Encounter founder through advisory work, see exit signals, suggest Capital8 as a resource, make intro if founder is receptive. No obligation to source, no quotas.

**What Capital8 does**: Everything. ExitFit, Sprint, process, buyer sourcing, deal management, legal coordination.

**Compliance Note**: Advisors often have non-compete or client exclusivity agreements with their firms. Track 3 referral agreements must explicitly address this. See Compliance Notes section below.

Example: Tax accountant meets founder in annual tax planning meeting. Founder mentions wanting to plan ahead for "eventual exit in a few years." Accountant recognizes signal, suggests talking to Capital8 to understand options, makes introduction. If deal closes, Advisor receives commission but with explicit carve-out for their existing client agreements.

## Referral Quality Standards

### What Qualifies

A qualified referral meets these criteria:

- **Revenue**: $2M minimum, $25M maximum (Dealmakers can go higher, to $50M, if the deal has strategic value)
- **Profitability**: Real EBITDA (verified or verifiable), minimum 15% margin preferred, no exceptions for high-growth at loss
- **Ownership**: Founder owns meaningful equity stake (51%+) and has decision authority to engage advisor and facilitate process
- **Exit Intent**: Genuine interest in exit planning within 12-36 months. Not "someday." Not "just exploring." Actionable timeline.
- **Advisory-Ready**: Open to working with Capital8 as advisor, not seeking buyer-only introductions or broker placement
- **Not Distressed**: No bankruptcy, no imminent crisis, no forced sale. Founder is in control of timeline and process.
- **No Broker**: Founder has not signed representation agreement with traditional broker. If they have, refer back to Connector about potential "second opinion" mandate.

### What Disqualifies

- **Public companies or heavily VC-backed**: Not in Capital8's lane
- **Under $2M revenue or over $25M EBITDA without strategic context**: Wrong scale for Capital8 model
- **Owner-not-interested**: Hired CEO or management team only — founder has checked out
- **Unrealistic valuations**: Founder is asking for multiples that are market-detached (expects 20x EBITDA in mature B2B services, etc.). Will waste everyone's time in valuation negotiation.
- **Distressed or crisis sales**: Business in Chapter 11, owner facing legal issues, imminent forced exit
- **Buyer-only searches**: Founder wants you to find them a buyer, not advise them. Wrong service.
- **Already under contract with broker**: Referral is invalid unless founder is looking for second opinion (which is valid — refer to Connector track for "second opinion" conversation)

### How to Brief a Referral Source

When onboarding a new network member, clarify the standard:

"A qualified referral is a founder you know who (1) has revenue between $2-25M, real profitability, (2) owns the business and can make decisions, (3) is genuinely thinking about exit in the next 12-36 months — not 'someday,' and (4) hasn't already hired a broker. That's it. If they meet those four things, send them our way. Don't sell them on Capital8. Don't convince them they should exit. Just make the intro and tell us a bit about their situation. We'll take it from there."

Emphasize what disqualifies: distressed situations, unrealistic expectations, or founder apathy.

## Compliance Notes

### Jurisdictional Variance

M&A referral fee regulations vary significantly by jurisdiction. Some states regulate referral fees strictly; others don't. Before deploying this program nationally:

[FILL IN: Sven to confirm which jurisdictions Capital8 operates in and confirm specific requirements]

Common jurisdictional concerns:

- **Texas**: Referral fees are permitted but must be in writing and properly disclosed to both parties
- **California**: Referral fees are regulated under Business & Professions Code and must comply with anti-fee-splitting rules in some contexts
- **New York**: M&A advisory and referral fees have specific documentation requirements
- **Federal**: If any referral involves an ESOP or securities transaction, SEC or state securities division may have rules

### Referral Agreements

Before submitting any referral, Capital8 should:

1. **Have a written referral agreement in place** with the referrer, signed by both parties. Template should cover:
   - Definition of "qualified referral"
   - Referrer's commitment (sourcing expectations, response time)
   - Capital8's commitment (follow-up within X days, feedback protocol)
   - Commission structure and payment terms
   - Confidentiality and NDA obligations
   - Clawback or holdback provisions
   - Dispute resolution

2. **Disclose the referral relationship to the founder** in writing before or during the first Sprint conversation. Founders should know Capital8 has a relationship with the person who referred them and understand what that means for confidentiality.

3. **File referral agreements in a central registry** so that if a deal goes through multiple sources (Dealmaker A sources, Connector B refers same founder), Capital8 can track and avoid double-commission.

### Track 3 Professional Restrictions

Advisors (accountants, lawyers, wealth managers) often have non-compete or client exclusivity clauses in their employment agreements. A referral agreement for Track 3 must include:

- **Carve-out clause**: "This referral agreement does not override any client confidentiality, non-compete, or exclusivity obligations Referrer has with their employer. Referrer warrants they have disclosed this arrangement to [their firm/employer] and received written permission to participate in Capital8's referral program."
- **Indemnification**: Capital8 indemnifies Referrer against any claims from their employer that the referral violated non-compete or exclusivity obligations (within reason).

Without these protections, a wealth manager or accountant referring a client deal could violate their employment agreement, creating liability for both them and Capital8.

## Activation Workflow

### New Member Onboarding (Days 1-7)

1. **Day 0**: Invitation email (from Sven or business development lead) with one-paragraph overview of the three tracks and what a qualified referral looks like.

2. **Day 1**: Onboarding call (15-20 min). Confirm track assignment, walk through qualified referral definition, answer questions, explain economics (in general terms), send referral agreement for signature.

3. **Day 3**: Follow-up email with:
   - Signed referral agreement
   - One-pager: "How to Spot an Exit-Ready Founder" (see email-sequence-capital8 skill)
   - Link to referral form or reply-to submit first referral
   - Calendar link for Dealmakers to schedule monthly check-in call

4. **Day 7**: If no activity, send gentle nudge: "Got the agreement signed? Any questions before you start referring?"

### First-30-Days Activation Target

Goal: At least one qualified referral submitted in first 30 days. This does two things — proves the network member is engaged and gives Capital8 data about quality.

- **Dealmakers**: 1 qualified referral submitted by Day 30
- **Connectors**: 1 qualified intro by Day 30
- **Advisors**: 1 referral opportunity flagged by Day 30 (may not close, just signal that they're looking)

If no activity by Day 30: schedule a check-in call. Diagnose: Are they busy? Do they not understand the criteria? Have they not encountered anyone who qualifies? Reset expectations or accept gracefully that they're not a fit.

### Ongoing Engagement

**For Dealmakers** (high involvement):
- Monthly check-in call (30 min) to discuss market, refine target criteria, review deal progress
- Quarterly business review to review referral quality, closed deals, economics, and adjust commission structure if warranted
- Real-time deal communication (email + Slack channel) on active deals they've sourced

**For Connectors** (low involvement):
- Quarterly email with market update and "here's what we're seeing" intelligence
- Check-in call only if activity drops below 1 referral per quarter
- Annual business review if desired

**For Advisors** (very low involvement):
- Monthly email with "how to spot an exit-ready founder" reminder and market patterns
- Check-in call only if activity drops below 2 referrals per year
- Confidentiality refresher once per year (emphasizing what you can and cannot share from your professional relationships)

### Re-Engagement Trigger

Activity metric: submissions and qualified referrals per track.

- **Dealmakers**: >90 days without activity (submission or communication) = re-engagement trigger. Schedule call, understand if sourcing environment has changed, adjust target criteria, or accept that they're dormant.
- **Connectors**: >180 days without activity = re-engagement trigger. Soft email check-in.
- **Advisors**: >270 days without activity = re-engagement trigger. Very soft re-engagement; these folks are busy professionals and sporadic referral flow is normal.

### Escalation Path

If a high-value network member goes dormant:

1. **First escalation**: Business development lead reaches out (not automated email) to check in personally.
2. **Second escalation**: Sven reaches out if member is known to Capital8 or is a past client.
3. **Final status**: Mark as "dormant but not removed." Don't delete them; keep them on the list. They may re-activate later if they encounter a deal.

## Content for Network Members

Network members should receive one piece of content per month to keep referral muscle sharp and demonstrate Capital8's market awareness.

### Monthly Content Types (Rotate)

- **Market pattern** (1x per month): "We're seeing buyer consolidation in [sector]" or "Multiples have compressed 0.8x in [space] in the last 18 months."
- **Case study pattern** (2x per month): "Just closed a deal where [founder profile] benefited from [specific structure]. If you see someone similar, worth a conversation."
- **Spotting signals** (1x per month): "Founders who say [X phrase] are usually 6-12 months from action. Here's how to respond."

All content is PEER voice. Treat network members as professionals, not as recipients of marketing collateral. No sales pitch. Just patterns and signals.

### Sample Monthly Content Sequence

- Week 1 (Tuesday): Market pattern email
- Week 2 (Tuesday): Case study pattern post (LinkedIn, also send via email)
- Week 3 (Friday): Spotting signals email
- Week 4 (Monday): Referral opportunity check-in or call (Dealmakers only)

Total: 4 touches per month, 3 informational (Dealmakers 4 with check-in), all PEER voice, all actionable.

## Metrics to Track

Capital8 should maintain a `dealmaker_network` database table that logs:

| Field | Type | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|
| member_id | INT | Unique identifier for network member |
| name | VARCHAR | Full name |
| track | ENUM (1, 2, 3) | Dealmaker, Connector, Advisor |
| company | VARCHAR | Organization or practice name |
| referrals_submitted | INT | Total number of referrals ever submitted |
| referrals_qualified | INT | Total that passed Capital8 qualification |
| referrals_closed | INT | Total that resulted in closed deals |
| submit_to_qualified_pct | DECIMAL | Referral quality rate (qualified / submitted) |
| last_referral_date | DATE | Most recent referral submission |
| activity_status | ENUM (active, dormant, inactive) | Engagement level |
| economics_paid | DECIMAL | Total commission paid to date |
| join_date | DATE | When member was added to network |

**Reporting on this table**:

- **Monthly**: "Active Dealmakers" (submitted referral in last 30 days), "Quality Rate" (qualified / submitted %)
- **Quarterly**: Network composition (count by track), commission paid by track, inactive member review
- **Annually**: Network performance summary, top-performing members, economics alignment

Rule: Share quarterly summaries with network members (anonymised, your own performance only). "You've referred 5 deals, 4 qualified, 3 closed. Economics paid YTD: $X."
