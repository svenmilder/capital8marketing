# Capital8 Copywriting Standards

## Brand Copy Rules (Non-Negotiable)

### Formatting and Syntax
Zero emojis in body copy. CTAs may use one pointing emoji maximum. Zero hashtags anywhere. No exclamation marks in body. Confidence doesn't need volume. Every performance claim requires a number or qualifier. "We've helped founders exit well" fails. "We've closed $240M+ in transactions" passes. Never use urgency language: "limited time", "act now", "don't miss out", "before it's too late".

### Forbidden Vocabulary
Do not use: opportunity, synergy, leverage, ecosystem, holistic, robust, excited, thrilled, game-changer, unlock, unique, revolutionary, innovative, seamless, frictionless, best-in-class, world-class.

### Forbidden Tones
Never lead with enthusiasm markers. Avoid: "I'm excited to...", "I'm thrilled to...", "Fun fact:", "Plot twist:", "Let's be honest...", "Unpopular opinion...", "Take my advice...", "So grateful for...", "Blessed to...". These undermine credibility with the Capital8 ICP, which is typically 45–65 with significant wealth and deal experience.

### Sentence and Voice Requirements
Average sentence length must be 8–15 words. Active voice in 75%+ of sentences. Precision over enthusiasm. Credentials backed by specific numbers. Direct diagnosis of actual situations, not generic value propositions. Language that respects founder intelligence and experience.

## Compliance Constraints

Capital8 operates in the M&A advisory space, which is regulated. Copy must not promise specific exit valuations or returns. Do not imply guaranteed outcomes like "you will sell for X multiple". Do not use testimonials naming specific deal sizes without explicit client permission. Do not make comparative claims about other advisory firms without substantiation.

Approved claim types are track record ($240M+ closed), process (off-market first, creative deal structures), and methodology (advisory vs. brokerage distinction). ## Institutional Tone Calibration

The Capital8 target seller responds to precision over enthusiasm, credentials backed by specific numbers, and direct diagnosis of their actual situation. They do not respond to marketing language that sounds like a startup pitch, urgency or scarcity tactics, generic "we're passionate about helping you" positioning, or buzzwords associated with the SaaS/tech world.

## Capital8 Brand Vocabulary

Use "exit" not "sale". Founders don't sell, they exit. Use "mandate" not "listing". Use "advisory" not "brokerage" or "agency". Use "positioned" not "listed" or "marketed". Use "deal structure" not "terms". Use "off-market" as a differentiator, not a descriptor. Always capitalise and link "ExitFit Score" to capital8.io/dealready. "Sprint" is Capital8's paid diagnostic session—always capitalised. "Dealmaker Network" always uses both words and capitalisation.

## Copy by Channel

### Website Hero Copy
Formula: [who you help] + [what outcome] + [what makes it different]. No fluff, no metaphors. One-liner plus sub-line. Example pattern: "We position founders to exit on their terms. Off-market first, always."

### Email Subject Lines
Factual, under 50 characters, no "RE:" tricks, no clickbait. Good: "Your ExitFit Score is ready". Bad: "You won't believe what we found". Good: "Sprint timing for SaaS exits". Bad: "Shocking insight about your business".

### Paid Ad Copy
Headline under 30 characters. Body under 90 characters. One specific claim. One specific CTA. Example: "Headline: Exit on your terms. Body: Off-market advisory for 7–8 figure exits. CTA: capital8.io/dealready."

### Deal Summary (Investor-Facing)
Lead with the opportunity in numbers, not a story. Investors want sector, revenue, EBITDA, multiple range, deal structure, and Capital8 involvement. Pattern: "$8M revenue, $2.4M EBITDA, SaaS, 4.2x initial offer, earnout structure, off-market sourced."

### LinkedIn Posts
