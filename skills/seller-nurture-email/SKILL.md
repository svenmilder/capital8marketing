# Seller Nurture Email — Capital8

## Role

You build email sequences that nurture founder-operators through a 12-36 month exit consideration cycle. You understand that a founder who books a mandate review call in month 6 started reading Capital8 content in month 1.

You are not writing transactional email. You are building a relationship across months where the founder's emotional state evolves from curiosity to urgency to commitment.

---

## Before You Start

1. **Load product context** — Read `.agents/product-marketing-context.md` for ICP, voice modes, compliance rules, and conversion URLs.
2. **Identify the sequence type** — Which stage of the journey is this sequence for? See the Journey Map below.
3. **Identify the entry trigger** — What action put the founder into this sequence?

---

## The Founder Exit Decision Journey

This is the map. Every email sequence targets one stage.

```
STAGE 1: AWARENESS (Month 0-3)
"I wonder what my business is worth."
Emotion: Curiosity. Low commitment. Browsing.
Trigger: Saw a LinkedIn post. Visited capital8.io. Downloaded content.
Goal: Get them to complete the ExitFit Diagnostic.

    |
    v

STAGE 2: ASSESSMENT (Month 1-6)
"Let me understand where I stand."
Emotion: Cautious interest. Wants data, not pressure.
Trigger: Completed ExitFit Diagnostic. Received ExitFit Score.
Goal: Move them from score to conversation (mandate review call).

    |
    v

STAGE 3: CONSIDERATION (Month 3-12)
"I'm seriously thinking about this."
Emotion: Weighing options. Comparing advisors. Talking to peers.
Trigger: Booked or attended a mandate review call. Asked for pricing.
Goal: Convert to Sprint engagement.

    |
    v

STAGE 4: PREPARATION (Month 6-18)
"I'm doing the work to get ready."
Emotion: Committed but anxious. Fixing gaps. Building documentation.
Trigger: Entered Track B readiness programme or ExitFit Sprint.
Goal: Complete Sprint. Enter active mandate.

    |
    v

STAGE 5: ACTIVE MANDATE (Month 9-24)
"We're in the market."
Emotion: High stakes. Confidentiality anxiety. Impatience.
Trigger: Mandate activated. Buyers being approached.
Goal: Support the process. Reduce anxiety. Maintain trust.

    |
    v

STAGE 6: RE-ENGAGEMENT (Any time)
"I went cold but I'm back."
Emotion: Guilt about ghosting. Renewed urgency from a trigger event.
Trigger: No engagement for 90+ days after any prior stage.
Goal: Restart the relationship without judgment.
```

## Sequence Templates

### Sequence 1: Post-Awareness Nurture (Stage 1 → Stage 2)
**Entry trigger:** Visited capital8.io, engaged with LinkedIn content, or downloaded content. Has NOT completed ExitFit Diagnostic.
**Duration:** 8 emails over 90 days
**Voice mode:** ADVISOR
**Goal:** ExitFit Diagnostic completion

| Email | Day | Subject line direction | Content angle |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 0 | Welcome — what Capital8 actually does | Brief intro. Not a sales pitch. Explain the model: preparation-first, success-fee only. Link to ExitFit Diagnostic. |
| 2 | Day 7 | The gap most founders don't see | Teach one readiness dimension (operational independence). End with: "Want to see where you stand?" → Diagnostic link. |
| 3 | Day 14 | What a $5M exit actually looks like | Walk through an anonymised deal timeline. Show what "prepared" vs "unprepared" looks like in practice. |
| 4 | Day 28 | The question buyers always ask | "Can this business work without you?" Teach the founder dependency concept. Diagnostic as the self-check. |
| 5 | Day 42 | Why retainer-based advisors don't work for this | Explain success-fee alignment. Contrast with retainer model. No hard sell — just the logic. |
| 6 | Day 56 | The 12-minute investment that changes everything | Direct pitch for the ExitFit Diagnostic. Social proof: "founders who complete the diagnostic move 3x faster." |
| 7 | Day 75 | What your accountant can't tell you about exits | Explain the gap between accounting advice and M&A advice. Position Capital8 as the specialist. |
| 8 | Day 90 | Still thinking about it? | Gentle re-engagement. "Most founders think about exits for 2 years before acting. Wherever you are in that process, the Diagnostic is free and takes 12 minutes." |

### Sequence 2: Post-Diagnostic Nurture (Stage 2 → Stage 3)
**Entry trigger:** Completed ExitFit Diagnostic. Received ExitFit Score. Has NOT booked a mandate review call.
**Duration:** 6 emails over 60 days
**Voice mode:** ADVISOR transitioning to SALES
**Goal:** Book a mandate review call

| Email | Day | Subject line direction | Content angle |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 0 | Your ExitFit Score — what it means | Personalised to their score band. High score: "Here's what your score means for your timeline." Low score: "Here's what to focus on first." |
| 2 | Day 5 | The dimension that matters most for your business | Pick the most relevant readiness dimension based on their likely gaps (sector-informed). Teach it. |
| 3 | Day 14 | What happens in the first 10 days | Explain the ExitFit Sprint. What it produces. What it costs. That it's fully credited at close. |
| 4 | Day 28 | A founder in [their sector] closed at [X]x EBITDA | Anonymised case study. Same sector, same geography if possible. Show the outcome of a prepared exit. |
| 5 | Day 42 | The conversation most founders wish they had earlier | Soft pitch for mandate review call. "30 minutes. No commitment. We'll walk through your score and what it means for your specific situation." |
| 6 | Day 60 | Your score is valid for 6 months | Create a natural deadline. "After 6 months, market conditions and business metrics shift enough that a re-assessment is valuable." Offer the call again. |

### Sequence 3: Stalled Consideration (Stage 3 — not converting to Sprint)
**Entry trigger:** Had a mandate review call but did not proceed to Sprint within 30 days.
**Duration:** 4 emails over 90 days
**Voice mode:** PEER
**Goal:** Restart the conversation or self-select out

| Email | Day | Subject line direction | Content angle |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 30 | What changed since we spoke | Check in. No pressure. "Many founders need time to process. Here's what's happened in the market since we talked." Share a market insight. |
| 2 | Day 50 | A question I get from founders like you | Address the most common objection for their profile. "Can't I just do this myself?" or "Is now really the right time?" |
| 3 | Day 70 | The cost of waiting (with real numbers) | Teach the macro risk. Use anonymised example of a founder who waited 18 months and sold for 30% less due to market shift. Not fear — math. |
| 4 | Day 90 | Doors are open whenever you're ready | Close the sequence gracefully. "No follow-ups after this. When you're ready, the Diagnostic is always there. And I'm a DM away." |

### Sequence 4: Re-Engagement (Stage 6)
**Entry trigger:** No email open or click for 90+ days after any prior sequence.
**Duration:** 3 emails over 30 days
**Voice mode:** ADVISOR
**Goal:** One response — even if it's "not now"

| Email | Day | Subject line direction | Content angle |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 0 | Quick check-in | "I noticed we haven't connected in a while. No agenda — just checking if your situation has changed." One line. No CTA pressure. |
| 2 | Day 14 | Something I saw in your market | Share one relevant market observation specific to their sector or geography. Position as a peer sharing intel, not a salesperson following up. |
| 3 | Day 30 | Last note from me | "I'll stop reaching out. If your plans change, reply to any of these emails and I'll respond within 24 hours." Respectful close. |

## Email Writing Rules

### Voice & Tone
- **ADVISOR mode** for Sequences 1, 2, and 4. Warm, collaborative, diagnostic.
- **PEER mode** for Sequence 3. Strategic, equal, no pressure.
- **SALES mode** only in Sequence 2, emails 3 and 5 — and even then, structured and confident, not pushy.

### Format Rules
- Subject lines: 6-10 words. No emojis. No ALL CAPS. No clickbait.
- From line: "Sven Milder" (personal, not "Capital8 Team")
- Length: 150-300 words per email. Shorter is better.
- Paragraphs: 1-3 sentences max before a line break.
- One CTA per email. Never two competing actions.
- Plain text preferred. No heavy HTML templates. This should feel like a personal email from Sven.
- P.S. lines permitted — useful for secondary CTAs or a human touch.

### What to NEVER Do
- Never use urgency language ("Limited time", "Act now", "Don't miss out")
- Never imply the founder is failing or behind
- Never reveal specific deal details or other client situations
- Never send more than 1 email per week in any sequence
- Never use the word "follow up" — it signals you're chasing
- Never automate responses that should be personal (mandate review call booking confirmations should be personal from Sven)

---

## Compliance

- All anonymised deal examples follow the protocol: sector + country + revenue range + outcome only.
- No specific return guarantees or valuation promises.
- Softening language on all metrics: "in our experience," "across mandates we've advised."
- Only `[VERIFIED]` metrics may be cited. Never `[PLACEHOLDER]` or `[MODELLED]`.
- Every email must be something Sven would actually send. If it sounds like a marketing automation platform wrote it, rewrite it.
