## Sequence A: Post-ExitFit Diagnostic Nurture (Non-Converted)

This sequence targets founders who completed the ExitFit Diagnostic but didn't immediately book a Sprint. They are 6-36 months from exit readiness and need education, not pressure.

**Day 0 — Score Delivery**

Subject: Your ExitFit Score: [Number]

Body: "Hi [Founder], your score came back at [Number]. Here's what that means: [Interpretation — e.g., 'You're in the green zone for exit readiness. Highest impact areas are working capital efficiency and customer concentration risk.'] None of these are blockers. They're the levers that move your valuation and your terms when you do sell. Over the next few emails, we'll walk through what each one means and how to think about prioritizing them. Sven"

CTA: Read your score interpretation [link to ExitFit Score dashboard / full report]

---

**Day 3 — Education: Buyer Perspective**

Subject: What buyers actually look for in your revenue range

Body: "Most founders overestimate what buyers care about. They assume it's all about growth rate or EBITDA. Here's what we see: buyers in your range [revenue band] weight three things at 60% of their decision: (1) recurring revenue and customer churn, (2) management depth beyond the founder, and (3) working capital needs. The other 40% is growth, margin, strategic fit. If your company is strong on those first three, a slower-growing business will still sell for a premium. If it's weak on those three, strong growth doesn't make up for it. Think about where your business sits on each, and we'll dig in next time. Sven"

CTA: None. This is teaching.

---

**Day 10 — Pattern Recognition: Anonymised Deal**

Subject: We just closed something similar to your situation

Body: "Closed a deal last quarter that might help you think through your own exit. Company like yours — [2-3 sentence anonymised profile that mirrors the founder's business: revenue range, industry, challenge, founder situation]. Owner had never sold before, wasn't sure if they were leaving money on the table with the offers they'd gotten. Turns out they were: we helped them restructure as a partial rollover + earnout, kept them involved in the integration, and they ended up taking home 30% more than the offer they had. The trade-off was they stayed involved for two years post-close, which they actually wanted. Not every deal looks like this, but if the pattern resonates, it's worth a conversation. Sven"

CTA: Book a Sprint [link to calendar]

---

**Day 21 — Cost of Waiting (Factual)**

Subject: Market timing — it matters, but not how you think

Body: "You asked about timing in your ExitFit survey. Fair question. Here's what we're seeing: valuation multiples in [Founder's sector] have compressed about 0.8x in the last 18 months. Buyers are still active, but they're pricing risk differently. If you were thinking about exiting 12 months ago, the same deal would have been worth more then. Conversely, if you've been putting off fixing working capital or customer concentration, fixing those now will matter more in a compressed market. We're not saying 'sell now or regret it.' We're saying: if exit is on your roadmap, it's worth being intentional about timing and sequencing, not reactive. Most founder regret comes from not knowing when they became exit-ready. Want to talk through when that might be for you? Sven"

CTA: Let's talk about timing [link to calendar]

---

**Day 45 — Direct Ask (One Question)**

Subject: Are you ready to talk?

Body: "I've sent you several things over the past month. The simple question: are you ready to have a real conversation about what an exit might look like for you, or are you still in the 'thinking about it' phase? No judgment either way. If you're thinking about it, we'll stay in touch. If you're ready to talk, let's schedule something. Which is it? Sven"

CTA: Let's talk [link to calendar] or reply to let us know you're not ready yet

---

**Day 90 — Re-Engagement or Graceful Exit**

Subject: Checking in

Body: "It's been three months since you took the ExitFit Diagnostic. Either you've moved forward with someone else (awesome, good luck), or exit planning got put on the back burner (also normal). If it's the latter and you want to pick it back up, we're here. If you'd prefer we stop emailing, just say so. No hard feelings. Sven"

CTA: Let's talk [link to calendar] or reply "No thanks, remove me from this sequence"

## Sequence B: Post-Sprint Nurture (Non-Convert to Mandate)

This sequence targets founders who completed a Sprint but didn't sign a Capital8 mandate. They are 12-18 months from readiness and need warmth without pressure.

**Day 1 — Recap & Recommendation**

Subject: What we discussed, what's next

Body: "Thanks for the Sprint. Here's my recap: [2-3 sentence summary of their situation and the highest-impact recommendation]. If I had to pick one thing to focus on over the next 12 months, it's [specific actionable item]. That's the one that moves the needle on both valuation and your comfort with the process when the time comes. Happy to check in again in Q3 and see how it's progressing. Sven"

CTA: None. This is a recap.

---

**Day 14 — Specific Action They Can Take**

Subject: One thing you can do right now

Body: "You mentioned concern about [specific issue from Sprint, e.g., customer concentration, working capital, management depth]. Here's something you can start on this month that won't cost much and will move you forward: [specific, actionable, founder-implementable step, e.g., 'Draft a 90-day operating plan for your top 3 customers. Not perfect, just real. It'll tell you where the actual dependencies are.']. We'll talk through how it went in a couple months. Sven"

CTA: None. This is advice.

---

**Day 30 — Sector Market Update**

Subject: What's happening in [Sector] M&A right now

Body: "Thought you'd want to see this. [Sector] deal flow in Q1 was down 12% vs. last year, but valuations stayed flat — that usually means consolidation is still happening, just more selectively. Buyers are focused on [specific buyer profile or strategic priority relevant to their space]. If you were thinking about exiting in the next 12-18 months, this timing could actually work in your favor. We'll have more clarity in the next quarter. Sven"

CTA: None. This is market intel.

---

**Day 60 — Soft Check-In**

Subject: Checking in

Body: "How are you doing with [the specific recommendation from Day 1]? If you've made progress, would be great to hear. If not, we can talk through what's in the way. Either way, still here if you want to pick the conversation back up. Sven"

## Sequence C: Dealmaker Network Activation

This sequence targets network members (accountants, lawyers, wealth managers, advisors) who have been invited to the Dealmaker Network. Their goal is understanding what a qualified referral looks like and building referral muscle.

**Day 0 — Welcome & Explain the Model**

Subject: Welcome to the Capital8 Dealmaker Network

Body: "Thanks for joining the network. Here's how it works: we have three tracks. Track 1 is Dealmakers — people who are actively sourcing and referring exit-ready founders. Track 2 is Connectors — people who introduce warm connections when you see someone who might be a fit. Track 3 is Advisors — people in trusted professional roles who spot patterns and introduce businesses that make sense. A qualified referral is a founder with $2M-$25M revenue, real EBITDA, ownership interest to sell, and genuine exit intent in the next 12-36 months. Not distressed. Not looking for a buyer only — they want advisory. And ideally, they're thinking about it but haven't signed with a broker yet. When you see that, let us know. We'll take it from there. More specifics coming. Sven"

CTA: None. This is onboarding.

---

**Day 7 — Successful Referral Story**

Subject: What a successful referral looks like

Body: "We got a referral last month from [Network member name] about a founder in [sector]. Founder had $8M revenue, strong unit economics, was thinking about selling but hadn't talked to anyone. We did a Sprint, got clarity on their goals, and are now working on an off-market process. That referral will likely close a deal that generates [approximate outcome — e.g., '$1.2M in advisory fees']. That's what we mean by qualified. Not everyone you know is a fit, but when you see that profile, send them our way. Sven"

CTA: None. This is proof.

---

**Day 30 — How to Spot an Exit-Ready Founder**

Subject: Here's how to recognize an exit-ready founder in your network

Body: "You'll hear these things from founders who are actually thinking about exit: 'I don't want to give it away.' 'I've had offers but I don't know if they're real.' 'I've never done this before.' 'My broker just wanted to list me.' 'I want control over the process.' If someone in your network is saying stuff like that, they're thinking, and they're probably not working with an advisor yet. That's the signal. Not revenue, not growth rate — the signal is that they're asking the question and feeling unsure. When you hear it, send them our way. Sven"

CTA: Introduce them to us [link to referral form or reply to this email]

---

**Day 90 — Re-Engagement**

Subject: Still interested in referring?

Body: "Haven't heard from you in a bit. Either the right founders aren't crossing your desk (happens), or you decided this wasn't worth your time (also fair). If you want to stay active, just hit reply and let me know you're still in. If you're done, no problem — we'll take you off this list. Sven"

CTA: Reply to confirm active or remove me from the network

## Capital8 Email Voice Rules

### Subject Lines
- Factual over clever. "Your ExitFit Score: 72" not "You've been waiting for this"
- No clickbait, no fake urgency, no "RE:" tricks, no multiple question marks, no ALL CAPS
- Specificity when possible: "What buyers actually look for in your revenue range" not "Buyers want this"
- Maximum 50 characters. Full stop.

### Openers
- Never "I hope this finds you well" or "I wanted to reach out" or "I'm writing to"
- Start with the point: "Your score came back at 72" or "Closed a deal similar to yours" or "Here's what we're seeing in market"
- Direct address: "Hi [Founder]" or "Hey [Name]" — not "Hello all"

### Body
- Length: 100-200 words per email. No exceptions. Ever.
- Tone: direct, specific, no hedging, no softeners ("just," "really," "quite," "sort of")
- Structure: opening statement, 1-2 pieces of detail or reasoning, CTA or graceful close
- No forbidden vocabulary: opportunity, synergy, leverage, ecosystem, holistic, robust, excited, thrilled, game-changer, unlock, unique, revolutionary, innovative, seamless, frictionless, just checking in, touching base, circling back, following up, reaching out
- Active voice 75%+. "We help founders structure deals" not "Deals can be structured for founders"
- No exclamation marks in body, ever. Periods only.

### Tone
- Same as LinkedIn: direct, specific, peer-to-peer, no corporate voice
- Teaching mode (Sequence A): diagnostic, not sales. "Here's what this means" not "You should do this"
- Pattern mode (Sequence B): recognition, not pressure. "This reminds me of" not "You need to"
- Activation mode (Sequence C): specificity, not recruitment. "Here's what to look for" not "Join us"

### Sign-Off
- Always: "Sven" — no title, no company name, no phone number, no "Best regards"
- No second sign-off (no "Cheers, Sven" and then another line)

## Segmentation Rules

**By ExitFit Score Band**:
- Red zone (0-33): Sequence A. Focus on education (Days 0, 3, 10). If no engagement by Day 21, graceful exit. Don't pressure.
- Amber zone (34-66): Sequence A. Full sequence. Timing message on Day 21 has impact here because they're not quite ready but close.
- Green zone (67-100): Sequence A. Full sequence. Direct ask on Day 45 is high-intent here; they're close.

**By Source**:
- LinkedIn inbound: Sequence A (Post-ExitFit). Founder has already self-identified.
- Referral (network): Depends on context. If referred for Sprint, use Sequence B after Sprint. If referred pre-Sprint, use Sequence A.
- Organic / website: Sequence A (Post-ExitFit).
- Post-Sprint non-convert: Sequence B. Don't use Sequence A after they've already talked to Sven.

**By Network Member Track**:
- Track 1 (Dealmakers): Custom sequence. Focus on deal patterns, closed deal economics, referral quality. Monthly market intel.
- Track 2 (Connectors): Sequence C. Focus on identifying signals, how to brief a referral, what makes someone exit-ready.
- Track 3 (Advisors): Sequence C modified. Emphasize confidentiality and compliance; they're referring from professional relationships.

Rule: Never overlap sequences. A founder in Sequence A stays in Sequence A. Once they move to Sequence B (post-Sprint) or Sequence C (if they join the network), they exit Sequence A permanently.
