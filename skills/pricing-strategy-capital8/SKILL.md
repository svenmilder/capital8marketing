# Capital8 Pricing Strategy

## The No-Retainer Positioning

Capital8 operates on a success-fee model. This is a core differentiator and must be communicated precisely.

### What It Means
Capital8 earns when founders exit. Not before. The success fee applies only at close, when deal value is final. There are no retainer fees, no monthly minimums, no engagement retainers. This structure exists because the founder's outcome is the only outcome that matters.

### Why It Matters to Founders
The success-fee model aligns incentives. Brokers earn whether the deal is good or not. Capital8 earns only when the outcome is right. A broker might position a founder at a lower valuation to close faster. Capital8's fee only grows if the valuation grows.

### How to Explain It
"We're paid on completion, not engagement. That means we're incentivised to get you the right deal, not any deal." Avoid: "We work for free until close" (inaccurate; the Sprint is paid). Avoid: "We're performance-based like no one else" (many advisory firms use success fees).

### What It Is Not
The success-fee model is not free advisory. The Sprint is a paid engagement. The founder buys diagnostic clarity, not Capital8's time. The success fee is a percentage of deal value at close, not a bonus or contingent charge.

## The Sprint

The Sprint is a paid, time-bounded diagnostic and strategy session. It is not a proposal or pitch.

### Purpose
Assess exit readiness, identify value gaps, and define the path to mandatable deal. The founder walks away with a written exit strategy brief, gap analysis, and recommended timeline. The outcome is clarity, not commitment.

### Who It's For
Founders who completed the ExitFit Diagnostic and scored amber or green. Also founders who arrived directly with an active deal. The Sprint is not for founders scoring red (not exit-ready).

### What Converts Post-Sprint
A founder who completes the Sprint and sees the gap analysis is far more likely to sign a mandate than a founder who attended a free consultation. The paid engagement creates commitment and belief in the process.

## How to Communicate Fees Without Undermining Value

Never lead with the fee. Lead with the outcome. Never compare the fee to what a broker charges. Compare it to the value created. The right frame is "Our fee is a percentage of what we create above what you'd achieve without us." The wrong frame is "We charge less than investment banks" or "Our fee is competitive".

Use the contrast framework signature: "Not because X. Because Y." Example: "Not because our fee is lower. Because what we create is higher." If a deal closes 18% above the founder's asking price, the success fee is a fraction of the premium created. This is proof.

### Public vs. Private Disclosure
Never publish specific fee percentages in public marketing copy (LinkedIn, website). Discuss in 1:1 context only. For public copy, reference the success-based model and outcome alignment. Reference specific deal outcomes (percentage above asking) as evidence of value.
