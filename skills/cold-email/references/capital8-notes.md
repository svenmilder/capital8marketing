# Capital8 Cold Email Reference Notes

These notes extend the cold-email skill with Capital8-specific outreach principles. Load `.agents/product-marketing-context.md` before writing any Capital8 cold email.

---

## The Three Cold Email Use Cases

Capital8 has three distinct cold outreach audiences. They share nothing except the domain. Never reuse copy across audiences.

### 1. Dealmaker Outreach (Highest Priority)

**Target:** M&A lawyers, corporate accountants, fractional CFOs, wealth managers, financial advisors, private bankers.
**Goal:** Get them into the Dealmaker Network referral track.
**Why it matters:** Dealmakers hold warm relationships Capital8 cannot cold-build. One dealmaker relationship can produce multiple mandates over years. This is the highest-ROI outbound activity.

### 2. Seller Outreach (Selective)

**Target:** Founder-operators of $3M-$20M revenue businesses in APAC considering exit.
**Goal:** Get them to complete the ExitFit Diagnostic.
**Why it's selective:** Cold outreach to sellers is sensitive. Most founders aren't openly discussing exits. Cold email risks being perceived as "someone knows I want to sell" — which triggers confidentiality anxiety. Use only when there's a clear trigger signal (peer exit, funding round, hiring freeze, sector consolidation).

### 3. Buyer Outreach (Inbound-First)

**Target:** PE firms, family offices, search funds, strategic acquirers active in APAC.
**Goal:** Get them to complete AcquireFit or apply for Mandate Desk.
**Why it's inbound-first:** Buyer outreach works best when Capital8 has active mandates that match their buy-box. Cold emails to buyers without a specific deal to reference are noise. Prefer inbound (LinkedIn PEER mode content, Mandate Desk marketing) and only cold-email buyers when a specific mandate fits their known criteria.

## Dealmaker Outreach — Detailed Principles

### Voice: PEER Mode

Write as a professional equal proposing a structured collaboration — not as a salesperson chasing a lead. The recipient is a lawyer, CPA, or CFO. They detect sales language instantly and it kills credibility.

**What it should sound like:**
- "You're likely the first person in the room when..."
- "The referral track is built for advisors who are already in the room."
- "Happy to send the one-page brief if useful."

**What it must NOT sound like:**
- "I'd love to connect and explore synergies..."
- "Capital8 is the leading M&A advisory platform..."
- "Don't miss this opportunity to partner with us..."

### The Central Fear: Reputation Damage

Every dealmaker email must address — implicitly or explicitly — the fear that referring a client to Capital8 could damage the dealmaker's own client relationship. This is the #1 objection and the #1 reason dealmakers keep referrals informal.

**How to address it:**
- Lead with preparation gate: "Every mandate goes through a 30-dimension readiness assessment before any buyer sees it."
- Emphasise staged disclosure: "Room 0-4 — confidentiality is architectural, not a promise."
- Commit to relationship protection: "We don't cross-sell, we don't re-approach independently."
- Show institutional quality: "Your client enters a process that reflects well on the person who referred them."

### CTA: Asset Offer, Not Call Booking

Dealmakers are busy professionals. "Book a 30-minute call" is too much friction for a cold first touch. The CTA should be:

**Primary CTA:** "I have a one-page dealmaker brief that covers the structure. Can I send it over?"
- Low friction — they just reply "sure" or "yes"
- Gives them something to review on their own time
- The brief itself becomes the conversion mechanism

**Secondary CTA (Email 2-3 only):** "If you have a specific situation in mind, I can walk through how it would work."
- Higher intent — for recipients who are already warm
- Still text-based, not a call booking

**Never use:** "Can we jump on a quick call?" / "Do you have 15 minutes this week?" / "Let's schedule a discovery call."

### The Economics Must Be Concrete

Dealmakers are numerate. Vague references to "attractive referral fees" mean nothing. Show the math:

- "$5M mandate, 5% success fee, 10-15% referral share = $12,500-$25,000 for an introduction."
- "$8M mandate = $36,000-$54,000 referral earn."

This isn't aggressive — it's transparent. It shows you respect their intelligence and you've done the math.

### Sequence Structure: 3 Emails, 3 Angles

| Email | Day | Angle | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0 | The proposition | Introduce Capital8, explain the referral track, offer the one-page brief |
| 2 | 7-10 | The fear | Address reputation risk and process quality. Second proof point. |
| 3 | 21-25 | The close | Respectful breakup. Restate the asset offer. Include a URL for self-service. |

**Each email must stand alone.** Don't reference "my last email" or "as I mentioned." They may not have read the previous ones.

### Personalisation by Role

| Target | Personalise Around | Geography-Specific |
|---|---|---|
| M&A Lawyers | Practice area, recent deals, firm positioning in APAC M&A | Singapore: reference SG corporate/M&A ecosystem, recent regulatory changes |
| Accountants | Client segment (SMEs), firm type (Big 4 vs boutique), service mix | Malaysia/Indonesia: reference SME succession challenge, family business dynamics |
| CFOs/Advisors | Client portfolio, advisory specialisation, thought leadership | Australia: reference AU-APAC cross-border corridor, direct commercial tone |

### Tone Calibration by Geography

| Geography | Register | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Singapore | Professional, precise, no wasted words | Lawyers in SG value efficiency. Get to the point. |
| Malaysia | Warm but professional, slightly more relationship-forward | Accountants in MY respond to relationship language. Add one personal touch. |
| Indonesia | Respectful, relationship-first, formal on first touch | Jakarta professionals expect formality in initial contact. Warm in follow-ups. |
| Australia | Direct, commercially explicit, slightly more casual | Australians appreciate directness. Lead with the economics earlier. |

## Seller Cold Outreach — When and How

### When to send:
- Trigger event observed: peer exit announced, sector consolidation news, hiring freeze, founder LinkedIn post about "strategic options"
- Warm signal: they visited capital8.io, engaged with LinkedIn content, downloaded content
- Third-party introduction: someone mentioned they're "thinking about it"

### When NOT to send:
- No trigger signal — cold outreach to random founders is spam
- Founder is publicly fundraising (wrong signal — they're building, not selling)
- Business is in distress (Capital8 doesn't do distressed deals)

### Tone for sellers:
- ADVISOR mode, not PEER or SALES
- Acknowledge the emotional weight: "Founders typically think about this for 2 years before acting"
- CTA is always the ExitFit Diagnostic: "12 minutes, free, no advisor call"
- Never use urgency language or imply they should sell now

### Structure:
- 3 emails max over 30 days
- Email 1: Trigger observation → ExitFit as a low-commitment first step
- Email 2: Teach one readiness concept (operational independence, financial normalisation)
- Email 3: Respectful close. "When you're ready, the diagnostic is always there."

## Buyer Cold Outreach — When and How

### When to send:
- Capital8 has an active mandate that matches their stated buy-box
- They've been active in APAC deal markets (public announcements, conference attendance)
- Warm signal: engaged with SevenX/Capital8 content, visited /buyers page

### Tone for buyers:
- PEER mode. Institutional. Numbers-first.
- Lead with the mandate (anonymised: sector + geography + revenue range)
- CTA: "Complete AcquireFit to access the full brief" or "I can send the Room 0 teaser"
- Never oversell the deal — buyers detect hype instantly

## Compliance — All Cold Emails

### Hard rules:
- **From line:** Always "Sven Milder" — personal, not "Capital8 Team"
- **Plain text only.** No HTML templates, no images, no tracking pixels in the email body
- **No guaranteed returns or multiples.** "In our experience" and "across mandates we've advised" for any performance claims
- **No specific deal details** in cold outreach to non-NDA recipients. Anonymised only: sector + country + revenue range
- **No forbidden vocabulary:** opportunity, synergy, leverage, ecosystem, holistic, robust, excited, thrilled, game-changer, unlock, passionate, innovative, seamless, frictionless, best-in-class, world-class, revolutionary, disrupting
- **Brand terms exact:** Capital8 (no space), ExitFit Diagnostic (capitalised), Mandate (not "listing"), Success fee (not "commission"), Mandate Desk (capital M, capital D)
- **"Advisory services only"** in email signature if space permits
- **Respect opt-outs immediately.** If they reply "not interested," remove from all sequences. No "are you sure?" follow-up.

### Sending infrastructure:
- Send from sven@capital8.io or a verified personal domain
- Warm the domain before scaling (start with 10-20/day)
- No purchased lists. Build from LinkedIn Sales Nav, conference attendee lists, professional directory research
- Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11am local time for recipient's geography
- Minimum 7 days between emails in any sequence

### Sequence exit triggers:
- Any reply (positive, negative, or "not now") → exit sequence
- Email bounce → remove from list
- No response after 3 emails → move to LinkedIn warm-up or re-engagement in 90 days
- "Not now" reply → re-engagement sequence in 90 days (not immediate follow-up)
