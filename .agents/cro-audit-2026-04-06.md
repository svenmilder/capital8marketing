# CRO Audit — capital8.io

**Date:** 2026-04-06
**Audited by:** page-cro skill applied against product-marketing-context.md
**Pages audited:** Homepage (/), Seller page (/seller), Buyers page (/buyers), Dealmakers page (/dealmakers), Investor Pricing (/investor-pricing), ExitFit Diagnostic (/dealready)

---

## Executive Summary

Capital8.io is a well-built site with strong copy, clear differentiation, and consistent brand voice. The site's primary weakness is not messaging quality — it's conversion architecture. The site does too many things well without forcing a single next action per audience. The three-audience structure (sellers, buyers, dealmakers) creates navigation complexity that dilutes conversion pressure for each group.

**Biggest conversion opportunities (in order of impact):**
1. Homepage audience routing — cold visitors don't self-select fast enough
2. Seller page has too many CTAs competing with ExitFit Diagnostic
3. Buyer page buries the free AcquireFit entry behind content-heavy sections
4. Dealmakers page is strong but the application CTA competes with pricing links
5. Cross-site: "Get Started" nav button links to /investor-signup — wrong default for a sell-side advisory firm

## Page 1: Homepage (/)

### Current State
- **H1:** "You built something worth selling well."
- **Primary CTA:** "Discover Your Exit Readiness →" → /dealready
- **Secondary visual:** Live deal ticker (MANDATE LIVE, IOI RECEIVED, DEAL CLOSED, etc.)
- **Stats bar:** $240M+, 4–9 mo, 100% success-fee, 10 days Sprint
- **Sections:** Deal flow carousel → How it works (4 steps) → Comparison table → Seller expectations → Who this is for → FAQ → Final CTA
- **Final CTA:** "Take the Exit Readiness Assessment →"

### What's Working
- **Headline is excellent.** Emotional, specific, seller-resonant. Passes the 5-second test.
- **Subheadline creates urgency through specificity:** "12 minutes understanding where they stand" vs "12 months finding out the hard way."
- **Comparison table** is the strongest conversion element on the page — makes the differentiation structural, not aspirational.
- **"Right fit / Not the right fit" section** builds trust by disqualifying — a premium positioning move.
- **FAQ section** addresses the exact objections from the product context (retainers, readiness, Sprint cost, confidentiality, success fee, geography).
- **Footer compliance line:** "Advisory services only. Not a licensed broker." — correct.

### Issues (Prioritised)

#### P1 — HIGH IMPACT

**1. Homepage defaults to seller audience but nav says "Get Started" → /investor-signup**
The H1 speaks to sellers. The deal ticker speaks to sellers. But the nav CTA "Get Started" routes to investor signup. A seller who clicks "Get Started" in the nav lands on a buyer flow. This is a hard conversion leak.
→ **Fix:** Change "Get Started" to audience-aware routing, or change it to "Check Your ExitFit" for seller-default. Alternatively, use the /dealready three-way chooser as the "Get Started" destination.

**2. Homepage tries to serve all three audiences but is 90% seller-focused**
The page reads as a seller page. Buyers and dealmakers get footer links only. A buyer landing on the homepage sees no path to deal flow. A dealmaker sees no path to the network.
→ **Fix:** Add a 3-panel audience router above the fold or immediately below the hero: "I want to sell" / "I want to acquire" / "I refer deals." Each routes to the right page. The /dealready page already does this — bring it higher.

**3. No testimonials or named social proof anywhere on the homepage**
$240M+ in transactions is a strong stat, but there are zero testimonials, zero founder quotes, zero buyer endorsements. For a high-trust, high-ticket advisory service, this is a significant gap.
→ **Fix:** Add 2-3 anonymised founder testimonials (sector + geography + outcome format per compliance rules) near the comparison table or FAQ section. Even "Founder, B2B SaaS, Singapore" attribution adds credibility.

#### P2 — MEDIUM IMPACT

**4. Deal ticker feels decorative, not functional**
The live deal ticker (MANDATE LIVE, IOI RECEIVED, etc.) is a strong trust signal but isn't clickable and doesn't lead anywhere. It creates curiosity without satisfying it.
→ **Fix:** Make ticker items link to the relevant audience page (e.g., "IOI RECEIVED" links to /buyers; "SPRINT STARTED" links to /seller).

**5. "How It Works" section uses step numbers but steps aren't clearly differentiated visually**
The Assess → Prepare → Position → Close flow is good content but the tab interface may not be obvious to scrollers. The content within each tab is dense.
→ **Fix:** Consider showing all 4 steps expanded by default on desktop, with the tab interface only on mobile. Each step should have one line of what the founder does and one line of what Capital8 does.

**6. Comparison table CTA is weak**
After a strong 10-row comparison table that clearly differentiates Capital8 from brokers, the CTA is "See Where You Stand in 12 Minutes →" — same as the hero CTA. This is a missed opportunity for a more specific CTA that leverages the comparison context.
→ **Fix:** "See How Your Business Scores →" or "Find Out If You're Ready →" — anchor to the fact that they just learned what "ready" looks like.

#### P3 — POLISH

**7. FAQ answers are long**
The Sprint FAQ answer is 60+ words. For a collapsible FAQ, answers should be 2-3 sentences max with a "learn more" link for depth.

**8. Final CTA says "No advisor call · No commitment" — consider testing without**
For a high-trust service, reducing friction is good. But "No advisor call" may actually suppress the highest-intent visitors who want to talk to someone. Test with vs. without.

## Page 2: Seller Page (/seller)

### Current State
- **H1:** "Before we find you a buyer, we make sure you're ready for one."
- **Primary CTA:** "Check Your ExitFit →" → /dealready
- **Secondary CTA:** "Learn About the Sprint" → /seller#sprint
- **Stats bar:** Same as homepage ($240M+, 4–9 mo, 100%, 10 days)
- **Sections:** Track Record → Why Exits Fail → Contrast (vs brokers) → ExitFit Score methodology → 10-Day Sprint → Mandate lifecycle → Pricing → Business fit → FAQ → Final CTA

### What's Working
- **"Why Exits Fail" section is the best content on the site.** Diagnoses 4 specific failure modes and implicitly positions Capital8 as the fix for each. This is ADVISOR mode at its best.
- **Comparison table** (Traditional Brokers vs The Capital8 Way) reinforces homepage version in a different format — list vs table.
- **ExitFit Score methodology** (Financial Integrity, Operational Maturity, Market Positioning) gives the diagnostic credibility.
- **Sprint timeline** (Day 1-3, 4-7, 8-10) makes the 10-day claim concrete.
- **Pricing section** is clean and direct: 4–5% success fee, no retainers, with an "Apply for Mandate →" CTA.

### Issues (Prioritised)

#### P1 — HIGH IMPACT

**1. Page has 6+ distinct CTAs all going to /dealready**
"Check Your ExitFit →", "Check Your ExitFit Now →", "Start Your Sprint →", "Apply for Mandate →", "Get Your ExitFit Score →", "Contact a Dealmaker" — at least 6 CTAs visible while scrolling. While they mostly go to /dealready, the varied button copy creates confusion about what action the user is actually taking. Are they checking a score? Starting a Sprint? Applying for a mandate?
→ **Fix:** Standardise to one primary CTA copy: "Check Your ExitFit →" everywhere. Use a consistent secondary CTA for higher-intent visitors: "Talk to an Advisor." The Sprint and Mandate are outcomes of the ExitFit process — don't CTA them separately on an awareness page.

**2. "Contact a Dealmaker" as final secondary CTA is confusing for a seller**
The page's final section offers "Get Your ExitFit Score →" and "Contact a Dealmaker." But a seller reading this page doesn't know what a Dealmaker is. That's a referral partner term. This CTA is either misplaced or mislabelled.
→ **Fix:** Change to "Talk to Sven" or "Book a Call" — direct and personal, which matches the brand voice.

**3. No case studies or proof of outcomes**
The "Track Record" section shows $240M+ and APAC coverage, but no specific deal outcomes (even anonymised). A seller at this stage needs to see: "B2B SaaS, Singapore, $4.2M ARR → Exited above asking with 4 competing IOIs." The deal ticker on the homepage has these — but this page doesn't.
→ **Fix:** Add 2-3 anonymised deal outcomes in the Track Record section, formatted per compliance rules (sector + country + revenue range + outcome).

#### P2 — MEDIUM IMPACT

**4. Page is very long — estimated 8-10 screen scrolls**
From hero to final CTA, this page covers: track record, why exits fail, contrast, ExitFit methodology, Sprint, mandate lifecycle, pricing, business fit, FAQ, and final CTA. That's 10 distinct sections. For a seller in early consideration (6-36 month window), this is comprehensive but risks losing the "12-minute diagnostic" simplicity that makes the entry point compelling.
→ **Fix:** Consider splitting into: (a) /seller as a conversion-focused page (hero + why exits fail + ExitFit CTA + testimonials + FAQ), and (b) /how-it-works or /process for the detailed Sprint, lifecycle, pricing, and methodology content.

**5. "E-commerce & Brands" listed as a fit category doesn't match the product context**
The "Is your business a fit?" section lists "SaaS & Digital," "E-commerce & Brands," and "Professional Services." But the product context explicitly lists "Consumer e-commerce, dropshipping, or content-only assets" under "Not the right fit" on the homepage. This is a messaging inconsistency that could erode trust.
→ **Fix:** Align sector fit across all pages. If e-commerce brands with strong defensive moats are a fit, say so specifically. If not, remove the category.

**6. "5,000+ verified APAC buyers" claim in the comparison section**
This appears in the "Data-driven matching with 5,000+ verified APAC buyers" bullet. This metric is not marked [VERIFIED] in the product context. If it's verifiable, add it to the context. If it's aspirational, soften or remove it.
→ **Fix:** Verify or replace. Per compliance rules, only [VERIFIED] metrics should appear on external pages.

#### P3 — POLISH

**7. "Aggressive preparation sprint ensure you are 'Buyer-Ready'" has a typo**
"ensure" should be "ensures." Minor, but on a high-trust page, grammar errors erode credibility.

**8. Sprint CTA says "Start Your Sprint →" but Sprint requires ExitFit first**
A seller can't actually start a Sprint from this page — they need to complete the diagnostic first. The CTA sets an expectation it can't deliver.
→ **Fix:** Route to /dealready with copy like "Begin with Your ExitFit Score →" — honest about the actual next step.

## Page 3: Buyers Page (/buyers)

### Current State
- **H1:** "Every mandate arrives prepared."
- **Primary CTA:** "Check Your AcquireFit →" → /acquirefit
- **Secondary CTA:** "Learn About Mandate Desk" → /mandate-desk
- **Live mandates section** with deal cards (Company names visible: Dominis Technologies, Project cezixige, wyjuti company)
- **Sections:** Live mandates → Speed Architecture → Market Problem → AcquireFit entry → Platform Intelligence (Room 0-4) → Buyer Profiles (4 types) → Mandate Desk pricing → Acquisition Finance → Geographic Coverage → Final CTA

### What's Working
- **H1 is buyer-perfect.** "Every mandate arrives prepared" — addresses the #1 buyer pain point (unprepared sellers) in 4 words.
- **"The Market Problem" section** directly names 3 buyer frustrations: unprepared sellers, misaligned intermediaries, no process visibility. Then: "Capital8 was built to fix all three." Strong.
- **4 buyer profiles** (Acquisition Entrepreneurs, Boutique PE, Family Offices, Corporates) with deal size ranges and what each needs — this is excellent buyer segmentation.
- **Room 0-4 visualization** makes staged disclosure tangible.
- **Acquisition Finance section** (20+ lenders, debt term sheets pre-LOI) is a strong value-add that competitors likely don't offer.
- **Geographic coverage map** with active vs partner markets is clear.

### Issues (Prioritised)

#### P1 — HIGH IMPACT

**1. Live mandate cards show company names — potential confidentiality issue**
Deal cards show names like "Dominis Technologies," "Project cezixige," and "wyjuti company." If these are real mandates visible to unauthenticated visitors, this may violate Room 0 confidentiality (which requires anonymisation — sector + geography only, no company identity). If they're placeholder/test data, they look unprofessional ("cezixige," "wyjuti").
→ **Fix:** If real: remove company names from unauthenticated view (Room 0 compliance). If test data: replace with realistic anonymised teasers ("B2B SaaS · Singapore · $3.2M ARR") or gate behind login. This is a compliance-level issue.

**2. "AI Deal Synthesis" descriptions are identical across all deal cards**
Every card shows: "High-potential acquisition opportunity presenting robust growth metrics and strategic market positioning." This is clearly auto-generated placeholder text. It uses forbidden vocabulary ("robust") and says nothing specific.
→ **Fix:** Either populate with real AI-generated summaries per mandate, or remove the AI synthesis line from unauthenticated view and show only sector + geography + revenue range.

**3. Financial metrics show "Undisclosed" on most cards**
Revenue, EBITDA, Growth, and Margin all show "Undisclosed" or "—" on most deal cards. For a buyer audience, empty financial data on a deal card is worse than no card at all. It signals either: (a) the sellers haven't provided data, or (b) the platform doesn't have real deal flow.
→ **Fix:** Show ranges instead of "Undisclosed" (e.g., "$1M-$5M"), or gate the entire deal card section behind AcquireFit completion so buyers see populated cards after qualifying.

#### P2 — MEDIUM IMPACT

**4. Mandate Desk pricing ($1,500 and $2,500/mo) shown on buyer page differs from product context**
Product context says: "Starter $3K/mo, Institutional $6K/mo." The buyer page shows $1,500/mo and $2,500/mo. The investor-pricing page confirms the lower numbers. Either the product context is out of date or the site pricing is wrong.
→ **Fix:** Reconcile. Update the product-marketing-context.md to match actual pricing, or update the site.

**5. Page is extremely long — 12+ sections**
Even more content than the seller page. From hero to final CTA: live mandates, speed architecture, market problem, AcquireFit entry, platform intelligence, buyer profiles, not-the-right-fit, Mandate Desk pricing, acquisition finance, geographic coverage, and final CTA.
→ **Fix:** Split into /buyers (conversion page: hero + market problem + AcquireFit CTA + 2-3 deal cards + proof) and /mandate-desk (detailed pricing, profiles, finance, geography for qualified buyers).

**6. "Not the right fit" section is buried below buyer profiles**
The qualifying/disqualifying section ("Browsers without a defined buy-box," "Anyone needing 9+ months to evaluate a teaser") is good but appears too late. It should appear earlier to self-select unqualified visitors out before they consume the detailed content.
→ **Fix:** Move "Not the right fit" closer to the top — either as part of the hero section or immediately after the live mandates.

#### P3 — POLISH

**7. "Complete AcquireFit to unlock AI Q&A and full dataroom access" — unclear value prop**
"AI Q&A" isn't explained until much later on the page. For a buyer scanning, "unlock AI Q&A" doesn't convey value.
→ **Fix:** "Complete AcquireFit to access full financials and dataroom" — what buyers actually want.

## Page 4: Dealmakers Page (/dealmakers)

### Current State
- **H1:** "You originate. We close."
- **Primary CTA:** "Apply to Join the Network →" → /dealmaker-signup
- **Three tracks:** Refer a Deal (10-15%), Co-Advise (70-75%), Build a Territory (override commissions)
- **Sections:** Three tracks with CTAs → Infrastructure → Deal Economics (4 worked examples) → Country Partner Programme → Why the Network Works → FAQ → Final 3-panel CTA

### What's Working
- **H1 is the strongest headline on the site.** "You originate. We close." — immediately clear value proposition for the dealmaker audience.
- **Three-track structure** is exceptionally well-designed. Each track has: who it's for, how they earn, worked fee example, and a distinct CTA. This is the best-structured page on the site.
- **Deal economics section** with 4 worked examples ($3M referral, $8M referral, $5M co-advisory, $12M co-advisory) makes the economics concrete.
- **"Your client relationship is protected"** — directly addresses the #1 dealmaker fear from the product context.
- **Country Partner Programme** with open territories and specific benefits is a strong growth mechanism.
- **"Application only · Curated network"** — premium positioning that builds exclusivity.

### Issues (Prioritised)

#### P1 — HIGH IMPACT

**1. "Submit a Referral →" (Track 1) goes to /submit-deal, not /dealmaker-signup**
The page hero says "Apply to Join the Network →" → /dealmaker-signup. But Track 1 says "Submit a Referral →" → /submit-deal. These are different flows. A visitor who wants to refer a deal but hasn't joined the network gets routed to a deal submission form — skipping the network application. This may be intentional, but it creates a tracking gap.
→ **Fix:** Clarify the flow. If network membership isn't required for referrals, make that explicit ("No application required for referrals — just submit the deal"). If it is required, route Track 1 to the application first.

**2. No proof of closed deals through the dealmaker network**
The page claims $240M+ in transactions and explains the economics, but shows no evidence of deals actually closed through dealmaker referrals or co-advisory. A professional considering attaching their reputation to this network needs proof.
→ **Fix:** Add 1-2 anonymised dealmaker success stories: "M&A lawyer, Singapore — referred a $5M B2B SaaS mandate. Closed in 6 months. Earned $X." Per compliance: sector + country + deal size + outcome only.

#### P2 — MEDIUM IMPACT

**3. Track 2 and Track 3 CTAs both go to /dealmaker-pricing, not a signup flow**
"Apply as Advisory Partner →" and "Apply for Territory Rights →" both route to /dealmaker-pricing — a pricing page, not an application. The CTA copy says "Apply" but the destination is "Pricing." This creates friction and unmet expectations.
→ **Fix:** Either route to an actual application form, or change CTA copy to "See Advisory Partner Pricing →" to set correct expectations.

**4. FAQ answers are not visible (collapsed)**
All 7 FAQ questions are collapsed. The questions are excellent ("Do I need to be a licensed M&A advisor to join the referral track?", "What happens to my client relationship once I refer a deal?") — but the answers aren't captured in the snapshot, meaning they may not be indexed by search engines.
→ **Fix:** Consider keeping the top 2-3 FAQs expanded by default, or add FAQ schema markup for SEO.

#### P3 — POLISH

**5. Country Partner section is long and detailed — may warrant its own page**
The territory programme, open territories list, and benefits section is substantial. For most dealmaker visitors (referral or co-advisory track), this content is irrelevant and adds length.
→ **Fix:** Consider moving Country Partner to /partners (which already exists in the nav) and keeping the dealmakers page focused on Track 1 and Track 2.

## Page 5: Investor Pricing (/investor-pricing)

### Current State
- **H1:** "Access to prepared mandates. Skin in the game at close."
- **Three tiers:** AcquireFit (Free), Mandate Desk Starter ($1,500/mo), Mandate Desk Institutional ($2,500/mo)
- **Acquisition success fee:** 0.5-1.5% at close
- **Equity conversion option** for success fee
- **Full fee summary table**
- **FAQ section**

### What's Working
- **Pricing is crystal clear.** Three tiers, one line each, with specific prices. No ambiguity.
- **Full fee summary table** ("Everything on one line. No surprises") — addresses the "hidden fees" anxiety directly.
- **Equity conversion option** is a genuine differentiator for search funds and independent sponsors.
- **Worked examples** ($3M at 0.75% = $22,500, etc.) make fees concrete.

### Issues (Prioritised)

#### P1 — HIGH IMPACT

**1. No recommended plan indicated**
Three tiers are presented equally. For most buyers, the question is "which one is right for me?" — and the page doesn't answer it. There's no "Most Popular" or "Recommended" badge.
→ **Fix:** Add a "Recommended" indicator on Mandate Desk Starter. Most buyers should start there. Institutional should be positioned as "For PE firms and family offices deploying at scale."

**2. Free tier CTA says "Build Your Profile →" but goes to /buyer-signup**
The AcquireFit section describes profile-building, but the CTA routes to a generic buyer signup. The distinction between "profile" and "signup" is unclear. Is AcquireFit the profile? Is there a separate signup?
→ **Fix:** Make the flow explicit: "Complete Your AcquireFit (10 min) → Access Deal Cards → Upgrade to Mandate Desk when ready."

#### P2 — MEDIUM IMPACT

**3. Equity conversion section could overwhelm casual visitors**
The equity conversion option is relevant for a specific buyer subset (search funds, independent sponsors). For most visitors, it adds complexity without relevance.
→ **Fix:** Keep it, but consider collapsing it or moving it below the fee summary table. Let the summary table be the anchor, with equity conversion as a detail for those who need it.

## Page 6: ExitFit Diagnostic (/dealready)

### Current State
- **Three-way chooser:** "What is your Primary Goal In the Coming 6-18 Months"
  - "Acquire a Business in" — Find & purchase high quality businesses
  - "Exit Your Business" — Prepare and sell your business for a premium
  - "Be a dealmaker" — Refer, originate or advise on deals
- No other content visible until a choice is made.

### What's Working
- **Three-way split is the right architecture.** This is the audience router the homepage needs.
- **Simple and clean.** One question, three choices. Minimal friction.

### Issues (Prioritised)

#### P1 — HIGH IMPACT

**1. Headline has a grammatical error**
"What is your Primary Goal In the Coming 6-18 Months" — inconsistent capitalisation ("Primary Goal In the Coming"). For the most important conversion page on the site, this should be flawless.
→ **Fix:** "What is your primary goal in the coming 6–18 months?" — sentence case, consistent.

**2. Subheadline is weak**
"Select the option that fits you thesis the best" — contains a typo ("you thesis" → "your thesis") and is vague.
→ **Fix:** Remove the subheadline entirely, or replace with: "This takes 12 minutes and costs nothing."

**3. "Acquire a Business in" — incomplete heading**
"Acquire a Business in" appears to be cut off. It should either say "Acquire a Business" or "Acquire a Business in APAC."
→ **Fix:** "Acquire a Business" — clean and complete.

#### P2 — MEDIUM IMPACT

**4. No trust signals on the entry page**
This is where all traffic is funnelled, but there's no social proof, no stat bar, no "Join 500+ founders who've completed the diagnostic." For a page asking someone to invest 12 minutes, there should be at least one trust signal.
→ **Fix:** Add a single line below the chooser: "$240M+ in transactions advised · 12 minutes · No commitment."
