## Product Overview

**One-liner:** Capital8 is a sell-side M&A advisory platform that prepares founder-operated businesses in APAC's lower mid-market for premium exits — on a success-fee-only model.

**What it does:** Capital8 advises founder-operators on the sale of their businesses. It is not a marketplace, not a broker, and not a SaaS tool. It is a structured advisory firm that uses technology to accelerate deal preparation and buyer matching. The core hypothesis: most founders leave significant value on the table not because they lack good businesses, but because they go to market underprepared, without competitive tension, and without the right buyer universe.

Capital8's model is preparation-first. Before any buyer sees a mandate, the seller has completed an ExitFit Diagnostic, received an ExitFit Score across 30+ readiness dimensions, and either passed directly into an active mandate (Track A) or completed a Sprint readiness programme (Track B to Track A).

**Product category:** Sell-side M&A advisory. Lower mid-market. APAC-native. Tech-enabled, advisor-led.

**Product type:** B2B professional services. Capital8 serves founder-operators (sellers), qualified buyers/acquirers (deal flow distribution), and dealmaker referral partners (network distribution).

**Business model:**
```
ENTRY POINT: ExitFit Diagnostic (free, 12 min, 30+ dimensions)
       |
SCORE >= 80 -> TRACK A: Sprint ($5K-$15K, 10 days, credited at close) -> Active Mandate (4-6% success fee at close only)
SCORE < 80  -> TRACK B: Readiness programme ($20K-$50K, score-banded, credited at Track A close) -> Track A
       |
MANDATE DESK: Qualified buyers access active mandates via Mandate Desk subscription (Starter $3K/mo, Institutional $6K/mo)
       |
CLOSE: Success fee collected. Milestone fees ($5K at IOI, $5K at LOI) credited against success fee.
       |
DEALMAKER NETWORK: M&A lawyers, accountants, CFOs refer deals. Earn 10-15% of success fee (referral) or 70-75% (co-advise).
```

**Revenue model:** No retainer. Ever. Revenue is success-fee aligned. Sprint and preparation fees are fully credited at close. This is the structural differentiator.

**Geographic focus:** Singapore (primary), Indonesia, Malaysia, Australia. Bangkok and Ho Chi Minh City as secondary markets. Cross-border corridor specialisation.

**Deal size:** $1M-$30M annual revenue businesses. Core band: $3M-$20M. Sub-$3M is inadvisable — inverted unit economics, shrinking buyer universe.

**Model inspiration:** M&A Research Institute (Sagami), Japan — adapted for APAC cross-border corridor dynamics.

## Target Audience

Capital8 has three distinct audiences. They must never be conflated in messaging. Each has a different job-to-be-done, a different emotional state, and a different entry point.

| Audience | Label | Primary Geography | Revenue / AUM | Entry CTA |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Audience 1** | Seller — Founder-Operator | SG, ID, MY, AU | $1M-$20M business revenue | ExitFit Diagnostic |
| **Audience 2** | Buyer — Acquirer / Capital Allocator | SG, Dubai, HK, Jakarta, KL | $5M-$100M+ dry powder | Mandate Desk Access |
| **Audience 3** | Dealmaker — Referral Partner | SG, MY, AU, ID | N/A | Dealmaker Network Application |

### Audience 1 — Sellers

**Target companies:** B2B SaaS, tech-enabled services, professional services (marketing, IT, outsourcing), B2B agencies, healthcare/clinics (multi-location), logistics and distribution, boring-but-recurring services.

**Decision-makers:** The founder. Always. Not a board, not a CFO. The person whose identity is fused with the business. Aged 35-55. Mostly male (70-80%), with increasing female representation in ecommerce, agencies, and clinics.

**Primary use case:** Exit planning, exit execution, or pre-exit readiness preparation.

**Jobs to be done:**
- *Functional:* Sell my business at fair value, cleanly, without leaks, to a buyer who won't destroy it.
- *Emotional:* Be recognised for what I built. Walk away with my reputation intact and enough money to change my next chapter.
- *Social:* Tell my peers and family I exited well — not that I sold cheap or got trapped in an earn-out.

**Buying window:** Considering an exit in 6-36 months. Most are "thinking about it" before they are "ready to act." The ExitFit Diagnostic is designed to convert thinkers into actors.

### Audience 2 — Buyers

**Profile:** Strategic acquirers (regional corporates, listed companies, family businesses), PE-backed operators and holding companies, independent acquirers (ETA / search fund model), and family offices deploying into operating assets.

**Decision-makers:**
- *Operator Buyer:* Mid-career owner-operator building a portfolio. Reads CIMs deeply. Decisive once fundamentals check out.
- *Financial Buyer:* Partner, CIO, FO head. Thinks in IRR, MoM, portfolio construction. Allergic to chaos. Buys systems, not just deals.
- *Strategic Buyer:* CEO, MD, Head of Corp Dev. On mandate to grow faster than organic. Wants synergy-obvious, integration-ready businesses.

**Primary use case:** Access curated, pre-qualified APAC deal flow without building an internal sourcing team.

**Jobs to be done:**
- *Functional:* Find well-documented, serious deals that survive diligence — without wading through noise.
- *Emotional:* Be the smart money that "got in early" on APAC lower mid-market before it gets crowded.
- *Social:* Deploy capital responsibly; maintain trust with LPs, co-investors, or family.

### Audience 3 — Dealmakers

**Profile:** M&A lawyers, corporate accountants (Big 4 and boutique), fractional CFOs, private bankers, wealth managers, financial advisors in APAC. Already in the room when founders discuss exits. Hold trust that Capital8 cannot cold-build.

**Sub-tracks:**
- *Refer a Deal:* 10-15% of success fee. Zero process involvement required.
- *Co-Advise:* 70-75% of success fee. Joint mandate execution.
- *Build a Territory:* Territory rights + override commissions on deals in region.

**Jobs to be done:**
- *Functional:* Turn existing client relationships into deal economics without building an M&A practice.
- *Emotional:* Refer confidently — not worry that Capital8 will underperform and damage their client relationship.
- *Social:* Be seen as a sophisticated connector in APAC M&A, not just a service provider.

## Problems & Pain Points

### Seller ICP — "The Tired Growth Founder-Operator" (Primary)

**Core problem:** Has a valuable business but no exit architecture. No clean dataroom. No verified numbers. No buyer universe. No process. Everything is in their head.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Traditional brokers: high retainer, slow prep (3-6 months), broadcast to large unqualified lists, no real APAC buyer network.
- Big 4: priced for $20M+ deals, institutional process that overwhelms.
- DIY: founder is emotionally too close, no buyer leverage, no competitive tension.

**What it costs them:** Leaves 20-70% of deal value on the table. Gets trapped in 3-year earn-outs. Sells to a single buyer with no alternatives. Waits too long past peak, sells in a worse macro environment. `[VERIFIED: Sven has observed 20-70% value gap between prepared and unprepared exits]`

**What disappoints them:** Advisors who take retainers and run slow, expensive processes with no close. Buyers who waste 6 months and don't move. Finding out, post-letter-of-intent, that their numbers don't hold up.

**Emotional tension:** Pride (I built something real) vs Fear (am I even sellable?). Freedom (I want out) vs Identity (who am I without this?). Urgency (market is peaking) vs Paralysis (I don't know where to start).

### Seller ICP — "The Legacy Family Owner" (Secondary)

**Core problem:** Emotionally attached to a business that needs a transition. No next-gen heir. Doesn't understand modern deal mechanics. Trusts only existing advisors, who often don't specialise in M&A.

**Why alternatives fall short:** Investment banks: too large-deal focused. Traditional brokers: feel transactional. Big 4: feel impersonal, institutional.

**Cost of inaction:** Business declines without a succession plan. Founder health deteriorates while the business runs itself. Estate complexity grows without early crystallisation.

**Emotional tension:** Legacy (I want the business to survive me) vs Control (I'm not ready to let go). Security (I need the money) vs Trust (I don't trust advisors).

### Seller ICP — "The VC-Lite / Tech Founder" (Tertiary)

**Core problem:** Cap table complexity. Multiple stakeholders with conflicting interests. Narrative is "growth story" but metrics may not support it.

**Why alternatives fall short:** VC-introduced bankers don't specialise in sub-$20M. Traditional brokers don't understand SaaS multiples or cap table dynamics.

**Cost of inaction:** Metrics deteriorate and deal value collapses. Runs out of runway and is forced into a distressed sale.

**Emotional tension:** Mission (I'm not just selling a business) vs Reality (the runway is gone). Reputation (I don't want to be seen as failing) vs Necessity (I need an exit).

### Buyer ICP — Core Problems

| Buyer Type | Core Problem |
|---|---|
| **Operator Buyer** | Can't find serious, well-documented deals in APAC without burning weeks on unqualified sellers. No internal DD team. |
| **Financial Buyer** | Too much noise. Non-standard financials. No standardisation across deal flow. Local intermediaries lack institutional discipline. |
| **Strategic Buyer** | Off-market lower mid-market deals are nearly impossible to find pre-process. Corp dev teams are small and stretched. |

### Dealmaker ICP — Core Problem

**Core problem:** Has warm relationships with founders who are actively considering exits, but no infrastructure to run an M&A process. Referring to a poor-quality firm damages their own reputation. Not referring leaves money on the table.

**Why alternatives fail:** Most referral arrangements with brokers are informal, unstructured, and the referral partner has no visibility on deal progress or quality of service.

## Competitive Landscape

### Direct Competitors

Traditional M&A brokers operating in Singapore, KL, Jakarta, and Sydney. Characteristics:
- Relationship-based, not process-based. No digital marketing presence.
- Typical model: upfront retainer ($10K-$30K) + success fee (3-8%).
- Typical timeline: 3-6 months to prepare, 6-18 months to close.
- No standardised scoring or readiness framework.
- Buyer universe is usually their own Rolodex, not a systematic market.
- Market is fragmented and relationship-driven.

**Capital8's edge:** No retainer. 10-day Sprint vs 3-6 months. ExitFit scoring on 30+ dimensions before mandate. Room 0-4 staged disclosure. APAC-native cross-border coverage.

### Secondary Competitors

**Big 4 Transaction Advisory (EY, PwC, KPMG, Deloitte):** Minimum deal size $20M+. Price-prohibitive for the $3M-$15M band. Institutional process; overkill for founder-operators. Capital8 explicitly does not compete here — it fills the gap below Big 4 deal minimums.

**Boutique investment banks (Singapore/HK/Sydney):** Serve $10M-$50M deals. Overlap at the top of Capital8's range. Relationship-driven. Retainer-first. Less systematic.

**Online platforms (Acquire.com, Flippa, Axial):** Primarily sub-$1M deals (Acquire.com/Flippa). Axial: US-centric. Not true competitors in the $3M-$20M APAC space.

### Indirect Competitors (Biggest Threat)

**"Do nothing" / founder runs the sale themselves:** The most common competitor. Founder convinces themselves they can manage an exit without an advisor. Result: single buyer, no competitive tension, lower valuation, longer timeline, higher leak risk.

**Waiting for a "better time":** Founder believes the business will be worth more in 18 months and defers. Macro risk, burn risk, personal health risk all increase with deferral. ExitFit Diagnostic is designed to interrupt this pattern.

**Existing professional advisors attempting to run the process:** Trusted but unequipped for deal architecture, buyer sourcing, or competitive tension creation. Capital8's dealmaker programme converts these advisors into referral partners rather than treating them as competitors.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators (structural core):**
1. **Success-fee only.** No retainer. Ever. Capital8 only wins when the founder wins. This is not just a pricing choice — it is the positioning spine.
2. **Preparation-first, not list-first.** Traditional brokers take mandates and send teasers. Capital8 scores readiness on 30+ dimensions before any buyer sees the business.
3. **10-day Sprint to market-ready.** Traditional prep takes 3-6 months. Sprint compresses it to 10 days, with a structured deliverable set.
4. **Room 0-4 staged disclosure.** Confidentiality is architected, not promised. Information is released progressively as buyers demonstrate commitment.
5. **APAC-native cross-border coverage.** Singapore-Indonesia corridor as primary. Not a Western firm retrofitting APAC.

**How we do it differently for each audience:**

*For Sellers (vs traditional brokers):*
- "I don't want to pay a retainer" -> No retainer exists. Sprint fee fully credited at close.
- "I'm scared about confidentiality" -> Room 0-4 means no broad disclosure before intent is confirmed.
- "I don't know what my business is worth" -> ExitFit Diagnostic answers this in 12 minutes with a scored output.
- "I've heard APAC deals are complex" -> APAC-native team with cross-border deal experience is the product.

*For Buyers (vs traditional intermediaries):*
- Every mandate has passed ExitFit scoring. No unscreened deals.
- Staged disclosure means buyers see clean, structured information — not founder-compiled packs.
- Mandate Desk provides ongoing curated deal flow, not one-off introductions.
- 48-hour exclusive first-look window for Mandate Desk subscribers on new mandates.

*For Dealmakers (vs informal arrangements):*
- Structured referral agreement with defined fee splits upfront.
- White-label ExitFit Diagnostics available for dealmaker's own client pipeline.
- Capital8 runs the process; dealmaker protects their client relationship.
- Three tracks with increasing economics as involvement deepens.

**Sellers:**

- Retail investors / individuals with no institutional structure or track record.

**Dealmakers:**
- Advisors who want to retain full deal control without co-advise collaboration.
- Advisors with no existing client relationships in the $3M-$20M business segment.
- Recruiters or HR firms referring "talent" rather than deal flow.

## Switching Dynamics

### For Sellers

**Push (away from "doing nothing"):**
- Burnout is accumulating. The business demands the same energy but returns less satisfaction.
- Market timing anxiety. AI, regulation, competitive pressure signals a "now or never" window.
- Concentration risk. 80-100% of net worth is locked in one private company in one country.
- A peer or competitor just exited. Optionality is now visible and concrete.
- A health signal (personal or family) reframes priorities sharply.
- Approaching a ceiling — intuitive sense that they are not the person to take the business to the next level.

**Pull (toward Capital8):**
- Success-fee-only model removes the "what if they don't close" risk objection completely.
- ExitFit Diagnostic gives a free readiness answer before any commitment is required.
- 10-day Sprint compresses what feels like a massive project into a defined, finite deliverable.
- APAC-native coverage solves the "but who are the buyers in this region" question.
- Staged disclosure architecture resolves the staff/confidentiality fear directly.
- Preparation-first model signals that Capital8 will tell the truth — not over-promise to win the mandate.

**Habit (inertia):**
- "I'll think about it next quarter." The business always needs attention right now.
- Existing relationship with accountant or lawyer who is trusted but not M&A-equipped.
- Mental model that exits take years and require a huge personal project to initiate.
- Identity fusion with the business. "Who am I if I'm not running this?"
- Previous bad experience with an advisor who took a retainer and disappeared.

**Anxiety (prevents commitment):**
- "What if the ExitFit score is bad news?" — Fear of finding out the business is worth less than expected.
- "What if the process leaks to staff or competitors before it's complete?"
- "What if I can't find a buyer and the process fails publicly?"
- "What if I get trapped in a complex earn-out I don't understand?"
- "What if Capital8 takes the mandate and doesn't close?"

### For Buyers

**Push:** Wasting time on unqualified deal flow from brokers. Frustration with non-standardised financials. No reliable APAC sourcing infrastructure.
**Pull:** ExitFit-scored mandates. Staged disclosure (less time wasted). 48-hour first-look exclusivity. Mandate Desk as a system, not a one-off.
**Habit:** Existing bank relationships or co-investor networks that occasionally surface deals. "We build our own deal flow."
**Anxiety:** "Will the deal flow be consistent enough to justify the Mandate Desk fee?" "Are the sellers truly committed to a process?"

### For Dealmakers

**Push:** Leaving referral economics on the table. Client asks "do you know anyone for exits?" and the answer is currently a weak referral or no referral.
**Pull:** Structured fee agreement. White-label diagnostic. No process involvement required on referral track. Capital8 protects the client relationship by running a clean process.
**Habit:** "I'll mention it informally." Keeps the referral casual to avoid accountability. Doesn't want to formalise something that might not close.
**Anxiety:** "What if Capital8 underperforms and my client blames me for the referral?" "What if the fee split changes after I've referred?"

## Brand Voice

Capital8 speaks like a highly competent, discreet private banker who has done this hundreds of times and will tell you the truth — even if it's not what you want to hear.

**Institutional confidence without arrogance.** Direct. No hype. No exclamation marks. Precision over cleverness. Earns credibility with specificity and real examples, not credentials and claims.

- **Nick Huber** (aggressive, no-BS, specific numbers)

**Style:** Direct, conversational at peer level, technical when warranted.

**Personality:** Precise, credible, measured, direct, discreet.

### Voice Modes (for Content Agents)

**ADVISOR mode** — Teaching, diagnosing, consulting. "Here's the pattern I've seen..." Lead with a specific anecdote. Use "we/together" language. End with a diagnostic question or readiness prompt.

**PEER mode** — Market insight, pattern recognition. "I've seen this across dozens of mandates..." Equal-to-equal register. Used for buyer-facing and investor-facing content.

**SALES mode** — Process transparency, methodology, direct CTA. "Here's what the Sprint produces..." Used to explain the system. Ends with a clear next step. Least used mode (~5-15% of content).

### Structural Signature

The contrast framework is the brand's most recognisable voice element:
- "Not because X. Because Y."
- "Most founders think X. What actually matters is Y."
- "They don't pay for revenue. They pay for predictability."

Use in 50%+ of long-form content. Never use in email subject lines or first-touch outreach.

### Copywriting Rules — Non-Negotiable

1. **Zero exclamation marks** in body copy. Confidence doesn't need volume.
2. **Zero emojis** in body copy. CTAs may use one directional emoji maximum.
3. **Zero hashtags** on LinkedIn.
4. **Sentences average 8-15 words.** Mix short declarative sentences with longer explanatory ones.
5. **Maximum 3 lines per paragraph** before a line break on social. Maximum 4 lines in email.
6. **Active voice 75%+** of sentences.
7. **One specific number per piece of content minimum.** Vague claims are forbidden.
8. **Post length 150-400 words** for LinkedIn text posts.
9. **Hooks under 15 words** (what shows before "...see more" on LinkedIn).

### Compliance Voice Rules

**Advisory services only. Capital8 is not a licensed broker in any jurisdiction.** This must inform all copy:
- Never claim specific deal returns or guaranteed multiples.
- Never make forward-looking statements about specific deal outcomes.
- All deal examples must be anonymised: sector + country + revenue range + outcome only. Never company name or founder name without explicit written permission.
- No jurisdiction-specific regulated financial advice claims. Singapore MAS, Australian ASIC, Malaysian SC rules apply.
- Softening language for metrics: "in our experience," "across mandates we've advised," "historically." Never absolute.
